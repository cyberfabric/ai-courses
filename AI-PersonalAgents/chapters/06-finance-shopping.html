Chapter 6

Personal Finance & Shopping

Budget tracking, deal finding, and subscription management — all through chat

A Bookkeeper in Your Pocket

In the last chapter your agent learned to control your home. Now it's time to give it a job that most people find deeply annoying: tracking money.

Think of your agent as a bookkeeper who rides around in your pocket. It doesn't have access to your bank account. It can't move money, approve payments, or see your balance. What it can do is keep a running log of every lev you spend, categorize it, watch your budget limits, and nudge you when things are going sideways.

The analogy matters: a bookkeeper is not a banker. Your agent tracks and advises. You make every financial decision. This is a feature, not a limitation.

What Your Finance Agent Can Do

  • Log and categorize expenses from a quick text message
  • Track budgets by category with real-time running totals
  • Alert you when you're approaching a spending limit
  • Extract data from receipt photos
  • Manage shopping lists with estimated costs
  • Track subscriptions and flag unused ones
  • Research purchases and compare prices
  • Split shared expenses and generate settlement summaries
What your agent should NOT do: directly access your bank account, store credit card numbers, make purchases on your behalf, or connect to banking APIs. Financial institutions have their own security models, and layering an AI agent on top introduces risk. Keep the agent as a tracker and advisor — not a spender.

Expense Tracking Through Messaging

The simplest and most powerful thing your finance agent can do: log expenses from a text message. No spreadsheet. No app. Just a quick message after you pay.

Just spent 45 lv on groceries at Kaufland
Logged! 🛒 Groceries: 45.00 BGN at Kaufland Date: April 8, 2026 This week's grocery spending: 45 / 150 BGN (30% of budget) Remaining: 105 BGN for the rest of the week.
Oh and 12 lv for coffee and cake at the place near work
Logged! ☕ Dining out: 12.00 BGN — Café Date: April 8, 2026 This week's dining out: 12 / 80 BGN (15% of budget) Remaining: 68 BGN.

That's it. Two texts, two expenses logged, budgets updated. The total interaction took 15 seconds. Compare that to opening a budgeting app, finding the right category, typing the amount, selecting the date, adding a note, and tapping save. The messaging approach wins because it removes all friction.

Natural Language Flexibility

Your agent understands natural language, so you don't need a rigid format. All of these work:

  • "Spent 85 lv on shoes" — categorized as Shopping
  • "Gas 60" — categorized as Transport
  • "Lunch with Sam 35 lv split" — logs 17.50 BGN for you, tracks 17.50 owed by Sam
  • "Monthly gym 50" — categorized as Health & Fitness, flagged as recurring
  • "Amazon 120 lv — new headphones" — categorized as Electronics/Shopping
Add this to your system prompt for reliable expense parsing: "When I mention spending money, always log it with: amount, category, store/description, date. If the category isn't obvious, ask. If I mention splitting, calculate my share and track the other person's debt."

Budget Alerts

Tracking expenses is useless if you only check the totals at the end of the month, when it's too late. Your agent should proactively warn you when you're approaching a limit.

# Budget configuration in system prompt
Monthly budgets:
- Groceries: 600 BGN/month (150/week)
- Dining out: 320 BGN/month (80/week)
- Transport: 150 BGN/month
- Shopping/clothing: 200 BGN/month
- Entertainment: 100 BGN/month
- Health: 80 BGN/month

Alert me at 75% and 90% of each budget.
At 100%, warn me clearly and suggest where to cut back.
Dinner tonight 55 lv
Logged! 🍽️ Dining out: 55.00 BGN ⚠️ Budget alert: You've hit 78% of this week's dining out budget (62 / 80 BGN). You have 18 BGN left for the rest of the week (3 days). Suggestion: consider home-cooked meals for the next couple of days to stay on track. Want me to suggest quick dinner recipes with what's in the fridge?

The agent doesn't just report numbers. It offers context (3 days left this week) and a practical alternative (cook at home). That's the difference between a spreadsheet and an agent.

Receipt Scanning

Sometimes you don't want to type every item. Just snap a photo of the receipt and send it.

[Photo of a grocery receipt from Lidl]
Got it! Here's what I read from the Lidl receipt: 🛒 Lidl — April 8, 2026 • Bread (whole wheat) — 2.49 BGN • Chicken breast 1kg — 12.99 BGN • Tomatoes 500g — 3.29 BGN • Rice 1kg — 2.99 BGN • Olive oil 500ml — 8.49 BGN • Yogurt x3 — 4.47 BGN • Bananas — 2.89 BGN • Cleaning spray — 5.99 BGN Total: 43.60 BGN Category: Groceries (cleaning spray moved to Household) Log everything? Or should I adjust anything?
Looks good, log it
Done! Groceries: 37.61 BGN, Household: 5.99 BGN. Both logged to your spreadsheet. This week's groceries: 82.61 / 150 BGN (55%).
Receipt scanning works best with cloud LLMs (Claude and GPT-4o are excellent at reading receipts). If you're using a local model, it might struggle with image processing. A practical compromise: use a cloud LLM only for receipt scanning and a local model for everything else.

Shopping List Management

Shopping lists are one of those things that should be simple but somehow never are. You forget to add stuff, the list is on the wrong phone, you buy duplicates. Your agent solves all of this.

Add milk and eggs to the grocery list
Added! 🛒 Current grocery list: Produce: • Bananas • Tomatoes Dairy & Eggs: • Milk (oat, for you) • Eggs (dozen) Pantry: • Rice • Pasta 6 items. Estimated total: ~30 BGN. Shall I share this list with Sam?

Notice the agent grouped items by store section, used your preference for oat milk (from your system prompt context), and offered to share with Sam. That's context working for you.

The "We're Running Low" Pattern

Add recurring staples to your system prompt so the agent can remind you:

# Recurring grocery items (auto-suggest weekly)
Weekly staples we always need:
- Milk (oat), bread, eggs, bananas, chicken breast
- Check if we need: coffee, olive oil, cleaning supplies

When I say "the usual," add all weekly staples to the list.
When I say "what do we need?", check what's missing from staples.

Shared Family Shopping List

If Sam and you both message the agent, you can build a shared shopping list without any coordination. Sam texts "we need cat food and toothpaste" at 2 PM. You text "add beer and chips" at 4 PM. When either of you arrives at the store, you text "what's on the list?" and get the complete, merged, categorized list.

No more "I thought you were getting the milk" conversations. No more shared apps that one person forgets to update. The messaging app you already use becomes the list.

For this to work, add Sam to allowed_users in your OpenClaw config and mention in the system prompt that both of you contribute to household lists.

Subscription Tracking

The average person has 8-12 active subscriptions. Many people are paying for services they forgot about. Your agent can maintain a subscription registry and flag waste.

# Subscription registry in system prompt
Active subscriptions:
- Netflix: 25.99 BGN/month (renews 15th) — shared with Sam
- Spotify Family: 19.99 BGN/month (renews 3rd)
- YouTube Premium: 21.99 BGN/month (renews 22nd)
- Gym membership: 50 BGN/month (renews 1st)
- iCloud 200GB: 5.99 BGN/month (renews 8th)
- Adobe Creative Cloud: 52.99 BGN/month (renews 12th)
Total: 176.95 BGN/month

Review quarterly. Flag any subscription I haven't mentioned using in 30+ days.
How much am I spending on subscriptions?
Here's your subscription summary: 💰 Total: 176.95 BGN/month (2,123 BGN/year) Potentially unused: ⚠️ Adobe Creative Cloud (52.99 BGN) — you haven't mentioned using it since January. That's 159 BGN spent since last use. Cancel or pause? Coming up: • iCloud 200GB renews today (5.99 BGN) • Adobe CC renews April 12 (52.99 BGN) — cancel before then to save Savings tip: If you cancel Adobe CC, you'd save 636 BGN/year. That's almost a weekend trip to Plovdiv for two.

Turning a monthly cost into a concrete alternative ("a weekend trip") is something spreadsheets don't do. Your agent makes money feel real.

Price Comparison and Deal Finding

Your agent can also watch for deals on items you're planning to buy. This is especially useful for bigger purchases where price fluctuations matter.

# Deal watching (add to config)
schedules:
  deal_check:
    cron: "0 9 * * *"  # Daily at 9 AM
    action: |
      Check current prices for items on my wishlist:
      - Roborock Q7 Max (target: under 400 BGN)
      - Sony WH-1000XM5 headphones (target: under 450 BGN)
      If any item drops below target price, alert me immediately.

One morning your Telegram buzzes: "The Roborock Q7 Max dropped to 389 BGN on eMAG — that's 70 BGN below the usual price and under your 400 BGN target. This looks like a flash sale. Want me to save the link?"

You didn't have to check any website. The deal came to you.

Splitting Expenses

Shared costs are a source of endless awkwardness. Your agent can track who owes whom without the "hey, you still owe me for..." conversations.

Dinner tonight was 90 lv, split with Sam and Mila
Logged! 🍽️ Dinner: 90.00 BGN, split 3 ways = 30.00 BGN each. Your share: 30.00 BGN (logged to Dining Out) Sam owes you: 30.00 BGN Mila owes you: 30.00 BGN Current balances: • Sam: owes you 30.00 BGN (from tonight) • Mila: owes you 55.00 BGN (30 tonight + 25 from last week's concert tickets) Want me to send a summary to Mila?
Nah Sam already Revoluted me. Mark that as settled
Done! Sam's balance cleared. Mila still owes 55.00 BGN.
For shared household expenses with a partner, track a running balance instead of settling every time: "Sam and I split household costs 50/50. Track a running balance. At the end of each month, tell us who owes whom and how much." This avoids constant micro-transactions and makes settling easy.

The Weekly Finance Summary

Every Sunday, your agent can send an automatic digest of the week's spending:

schedules:

    cron: "0 19 * * 0"  # Sunday at 7 PM

Financial Data Is Extremely Sensitive

Your spending data reveals your habits, location, health conditions (pharmacy purchases), relationships (gifts, shared meals), and lifestyle. Treat it accordingly:

  • Store financial logs locally. Use a local spreadsheet or self-hosted Notion. Don't rely on cloud services you don't control
  • Use a local LLM for finance tracking if possible. Sending "I spent 200 BGN at the pharmacy" to a cloud API creates a record outside your control
  • Never give your agent bank credentials. Not API keys, not passwords, not OAuth tokens. The agent tracks what you tell it, period
  • Be careful with receipt photos. Receipt images sent through a cloud LLM are processed on remote servers. If privacy is critical, use a local OCR tool first and send only the text
  • Encrypt your expense data. If your laptop or server is stolen, financial logs should not be readable without a password

Common Pitfalls (and How to Avoid Them)

Pitfall What Happens Fix
Forgetting to log expenses Budget tracking becomes inaccurate Set a daily evening reminder: "Did you forget to log anything today?"
Inconsistent categories "Coffee" goes to Groceries one day, Dining Out the next Define categories explicitly in your prompt with examples
Shared expenses getting messy Debts pile up, no one settles Monthly settlement reminder: "Here's who owes whom this month"
Budget too strict at first You stop tracking because you feel judged Start with observation mode — just log, no limits, for 2 weeks. Set budgets based on actual patterns
Agent judges your spending Feels like a nagging parent Add to prompt: "Never judge or moralize about my spending. Report facts and trends only."

Exercise: Set Up Your Personal Finance Agent

Get your money tracking running in under 30 minutes:

  1. Add the finance prompt section above to your config.yaml system prompt (adjust categories and amounts for your life)
  2. Log 5 recent expenses from memory:
    • Today's lunch
    • Last grocery trip
    • Last online purchase
    • Last transportation cost
    • Last entertainment expense
  3. Ask for a summary: "How am I doing this week?" — verify the totals make sense
  4. Test a shopping list: "Start a grocery list with the usual" — verify it knows your staples
  5. Test a split: "Dinner was 70 lv, split with [friend name]" — verify it calculates correctly
  6. Send a receipt photo (if using a cloud LLM) — verify it reads the items correctly
  7. Ask about subscriptions: "How much am I paying monthly for subscriptions?" — verify the total

Once all 7 steps work, commit to logging expenses for one full week. After 7 days, ask for a weekly summary. You'll be surprised what you learn about your spending.
