Calendar, Email & Communication

Memory Map
💡

The Big Idea

You pay a hidden communication tax of 2-3 hours daily — scheduling, email scanning, coordinating plans. Your agent is an executive assistant who reads 40 emails, surfaces the 4 that matter, drafts replies in your voice, and manages your calendar. One chat thread replaces Gmail, Calendar, and group chat coordination.

38 emails processed, 3 replies sent, calendar updated — 30 seconds total.

📊 The Four-Tier Triage System

Tier What Goes Here Agent Action
🔴 URGENT Family emergencies, flight changes, bills due in 3 days Show immediately, suggest next steps
📩 NEEDS REPLY Personal messages expecting a response, appointment confirmations Show with draft reply in your tone
📋 FYI Shipping updates, newsletters you read, social notifications Show count only ("8 FYI items")
🗑️ SKIP Marketing, promotions, spam, automated noise Don't even mention

✈️ How a Trip Cascades Through Your Calendar

  1. Trip Added

    "Vienna April 15-17" → all-day event blocked, marked unavailable.

  2. Day Before

    Check-in reminder, packing list based on weather, pet sitter notification.

  3. Travel Morning

    Alarm adjusted for early flight, taxi reminder, airport departure time.

  4. During Trip

    Non-urgent emails held, auto-replies active, only emergencies get through.

  5. Return

    Catch-up digest of held messages, calendar back to normal.

📖 Key Terms

Communication Tax
The 2-3 hours daily spent on scheduling, email scanning, and social coordination — not meaningful conversations, just logistics.
Email Triage
Agent reads all emails, categorizes into Urgent / Needs Reply / FYI / Skip, and presents a prioritized summary with draft replies.
App Password
A special token for Gmail/Outlook that grants only email access, not full account access. Use this instead of your real password.
Context Bridge
The agent's ability to connect information across platforms — an email triggers calendar, weather, and finance checks simultaneously.
Protected Time Slots
Recurring events (gym, book club) the agent will never schedule over unless explicitly told to.
Personal CRM
A contact database enriched with birthdays, gift history, relationship context, and proactive reminders.
Quiet Hours
Time blocks where all non-emergency notifications are held for the morning digest. Content-aware, not sender-based.
Focus Mode
A 2-hour block where ALL notifications are held. Summary delivered when the block ends.

🧪 Quick Quiz

Why should the agent NEVER send an email without your approval?

A poorly-worded reply sent automatically can damage relationships. The agent drafts replies, but you always review and approve with "send" or edits. The human stays in the loop for every outgoing message.

What makes quiet hours "content-aware" rather than "sender-based"?

Instead of blocking/allowing by sender, the agent analyzes message content. Mom saying "Great dinner!" at 11 PM gets held. Mom saying "Dad fell, hospital" at 11 PM gets through immediately. Same sender, different urgency.

What is the "context bridge" and why is it powerful?

It's the agent connecting data across email, calendar, contacts, and finance in one action. Stefan's "coffee tomorrow?" email triggers: calendar check → draft reply with free slot → tentative event → weather check → expense reminder that you owe him 25 BGN.

Why use App Passwords instead of your real email password?

App Passwords grant only email access (IMAP/SMTP), not full account access. If compromised, you revoke just the App Password without changing your main password or losing access to other Google services.

How does the agent handle social coordination across multiple people?

You say "dinner with Mila and Stefan this weekend." Agent checks your calendar, drafts a group message asking their availability, monitors replies, identifies the overlap, and suggests restaurant options — 3 messages from you total.

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • The communication tax costs 2-3 hours daily — your agent eliminates the logistics, not the meaningful conversations.
  • Email triage turns 40 emails into 4 actions: Urgent first, Needs Reply with drafts, FYI count, Skip hidden.
  • Draft replies must match YOUR voice per relationship — define tone rules for family, friends, acquaintances, and service providers.
  • NEVER send without approval. One-word approval ("send") is fine, but the human is always in the loop.
  • Calendar management includes travel time, conflict detection, protected slots, and preparation reminders — not just event creation.
  • The context bridge is the killer feature: one message triggers actions across email, calendar, contacts, and finance.
  • Communication boundaries (quiet hours, focus mode, vacation mode) use content analysis, not just sender filtering.
  • Use App Passwords for email, never your real password. Email content sent to cloud LLMs leaves your control.
