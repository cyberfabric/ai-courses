Learning, Media & Travel

Memory Map

The Big Idea

Your agent is three roles at once: a librarian who remembers every book and article; a critic who learns your taste and recommends what you'll actually enjoy; and a travel agent who turns "wouldn't it be cool to visit Japan" into a day-by-day itinerary under budget.

🎭 The Three Roles

📚
Librarian
Logs books, articles, podcasts, courses. Tracks yearly reading goals, maintains to-read lists, cross-references friend recommendations. Summarizes articles on demand.
🎬
Critic
Learns your taste from 8+ rated items. Recommends across media types, checks your subscriptions first, balances preferences for joint viewing with Sam.
✈️
Travel Agent
Builds complete itineraries from a 3-sentence brief: dates, budget, interests. Handles logistics, packing, real-time assistance during trips, and post-trip summaries.
🎓
Tutor
Creates structured learning plans with daily milestones, mixed resources, streak tracking, and pace adjustment. "Learn Spanish in 15 min/day" → 12-week curriculum.
📰
Summarizer
Send a URL → get key points in under 100 words. Connects articles to your personal data ("your sleep average aligns with this"). Saves to Obsidian.
🧠
Second Brain
Everything flows to Obsidian or Notion: book notes, trip journals, article summaries, media ratings. Builds a searchable personal archive over time.

🗺️ From Dream to Itinerary to Memories

  1. Dream

    "Japan in October, 10 days, 5000 BGN/person" — three sentences start it all.

  2. Plan

    Agent asks about pace, focus, must-sees. Builds day-by-day itinerary with budget breakdown.

  3. Prepare

    3 days before: packing list based on weather, activities, and your "always forget" items. Calendar events added.

  4. Travel

    Real-time companion: restaurant suggestions, translations, rainy-day shuffles, expense logging in local currency.

  5. Remember

    Post-trip: expense summary, trip journal in Obsidian, rating saved, recommendations ready for friends.

🧪 Quick Quiz

What three roles does your agent play in this chapter?

Librarian (remembers every book, article, podcast), Critic (learns your taste and recommends), and Travel Agent (turns dream trips into detailed itineraries with budgets).

How does the agent create a travel itinerary from just 3 sentences?

You provide destination, dates, and budget. The agent asks clarifying questions (pace, focus, must-sees, partner priorities), then synthesizes weather, logistics, accommodation, food, and activities into a day-by-day plan with cost breakdown.

What makes the agent's recommendations better than "popular on Netflix"?

It builds a taste profile from your 8+ rated items, avoids genres you dislike, checks your subscriptions first, balances joint preferences (you + Sam), and cross-references across media types (a book rating informs show recommendations).

Why is travel data a privacy concern?

Your itinerary reveals exactly when your home is empty, your travel patterns, financial capacity, and personal interests. Keep travel plans local and never share detailed schedules on social media.

What's the "post-trip loop" and why does it matter?

After returning: expense summary, trip journal compiled from chat messages, rating saved for future planning, and personalized recommendations generated for friends who ask. It turns a trip from a memory into reusable data.

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • Your agent is librarian + critic + travel agent — three roles in one chat thread.
  • Book/media logging builds a taste profile over time. Recommendations improve the more you rate.
  • Article summarization + personal data connection turns any URL into actionable insight in seconds.
  • Learning paths are structured curricula, not search results — daily milestones, mixed resources, streak tracking.
  • Travel planning goes from 3 sentences to a complete itinerary with budget, logistics, and both partners' interests balanced.
  • During trips, the agent is a real-time companion: restaurants, translations, weather shuffles, expense logging.
  • Cross-domain thinking is the killer feature: a TV show rating triggers book recommendations AND a travel nudge.
  • Your taste profile, reading list, and travel plans are surprisingly revealing — keep this data local in Obsidian.
