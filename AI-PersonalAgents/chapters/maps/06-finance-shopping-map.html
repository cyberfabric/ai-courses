The Big Idea

Your agent is a bookkeeper, not a banker. It tracks, categorizes, and advises — but never touches your bank account or makes financial decisions for you. Zero-friction text logging beats any budgeting app because it removes every barrier between "I just paid" and "it's tracked."

🧩 What Your Finance Agent Does

💬
Text-Based Logging
Natural language input: "Spent 45 lv on groceries" or just "Gas 60." Agent parses amount, category, store, and date automatically.
📊
Budget Tracking
Running totals by category with weekly/monthly limits. Alerts at 75%, 90%, and 100% — with practical alternatives when you're close.
🧾
Receipt Scanning
Snap a photo, agent extracts every item. Smart categorization splits "cleaning spray" from "groceries" into the right buckets.
🛒
Shopping Lists
Grouped by store section, estimated totals, shared between household members. "The usual" adds all weekly staples in one message.
🔄
Subscription Tracking
Registry of all subscriptions with renewal dates. Flags unused services and converts savings to real alternatives ("that's a weekend trip").
🔍
Purchase Research
Agent researches options within your budget and use case. Includes price, key features, drawbacks, and smart home compatibility.
🏷️
Deal Watching
Daily price checks on wishlist items. Alerts you when something drops below your target price — the deal comes to you.
➗
Expense Splitting
Auto-calculates shares, tracks who owes whom, maintains running balances. Monthly settlement summaries end the awkwardness.

🚀 The On-Ramp: From Zero to Budget Tracking

  1. Week 1: Observe

    Just log expenses — no budgets, no limits. Learn what you actually spend.

  2. Week 2: Categorize

    Review categories. Refine your prompt so "coffee" always goes to the same bucket.

  3. Week 3: Budget

    Set realistic limits based on Week 1-2 actuals. Enable 75%/90%/100% alerts.

  4. Week 4: Automate

    Add weekly summaries, subscription tracking, shopping lists, and deal watching.

📖 Key Terms

Budget Alert
Proactive notification when spending reaches 75%, 90%, or 100% of a category limit.
Receipt Scanning
Sending a receipt photo for the agent to extract items, amounts, and categories via vision LLM.
Running Balance
Ongoing debt tracker between people who frequently split costs — settled monthly instead of per-transaction.
Subscription Registry
A list of active subscriptions with costs, renewal dates, and usage tracking to flag waste.
Observation Mode
The recommended first step: log everything for 2 weeks with no budget limits, then set targets based on real data.
Weekly Staples
Recurring grocery items defined in your prompt. "The usual" adds them all at once.
Deal Watching
Scheduled daily price checks on wishlist items, alerting you when prices drop below your target.

🧪 Quick Quiz

Why is the agent called a "bookkeeper, not a banker"?

The agent tracks and categorizes spending you tell it about, and gives advice. It should never have direct bank access, store card numbers, or make financial transactions. You make every spending decision.

What's the recommended approach for setting your first budget limits?

Start with 2 weeks of observation mode — just log expenses with no limits. Then set budgets based on your actual spending patterns. Starting too strict makes you quit.

How does the agent make subscription costs feel real?

It converts abstract monthly costs to concrete alternatives. "Canceling Adobe CC saves 636 BGN/year — that's a weekend trip to Plovdiv for two." Spreadsheets show numbers; agents show meaning.

Why should receipt photos be handled carefully from a privacy perspective?

Receipt images sent to cloud LLMs are processed on remote servers, revealing purchase details and locations. For maximum privacy, use local OCR first and send only the extracted text to the agent.

How do shared shopping lists work between household members?

Both people message the same agent. Sam adds "cat food" at 2 PM, you add "beer" at 4 PM. Either person texts "what's on the list?" at the store and gets the complete, merged, categorized list.

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • Your finance agent is a bookkeeper, not a banker — it tracks and advises, never touches your bank.
  • Text-based expense logging takes 15 seconds vs. 2 minutes in a budgeting app — zero friction wins.
  • Start with observation mode (2 weeks, no limits), then set budgets based on real spending data.
  • Budget alerts at 75/90/100% with practical alternatives ("cook at home") beat end-of-month surprises.
  • Subscription tracking flags unused services and converts savings to concrete alternatives you actually want.
  • Shared shopping lists through messaging end "I thought you were getting the milk" forever.
  • Financial data is extremely sensitive — store locally, encrypt, never give the agent bank credentials.
  • Consistent tracking (10-15% spending reduction) beats strict budgets you abandon after a week.
