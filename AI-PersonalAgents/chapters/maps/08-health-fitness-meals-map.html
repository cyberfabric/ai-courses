🧪 Quick Quiz

Why do health apps have a 96% drop-off rate, and how does an agent avoid it?

Friction. Health apps require opening a dedicated app, navigating menus, and entering precise data. An agent uses your existing messaging app — logging is a one-line text that takes 10 seconds, not 3 minutes.

What six dimensions does the agent use when creating a meal plan?

1) What you already have in the fridge, 2) dietary restrictions, 3) grocery budget, 4) weekly schedule (fast meals on busy nights), 5) household members' needs, 6) cooking time preferences.

Why should health data use a local LLM rather than a cloud API?

Health data (weight, medications, symptoms, blood pressure) is the most sensitive personal data. Sending it to a cloud API creates health records outside your control that could affect insurance, employment, or relationships if exposed.

What's medication "escalation" and when should you use it?

When a critical medication isn't confirmed within a set time, the agent alerts another household member. Used for high-priority meds (like Pixel's twice-daily medication) where missing a dose has real consequences.

How can you mix coach personas for different health areas?

Set different tones per domain: "Encouraging for exercise, data-driven for nutrition." This gives you positive reinforcement for showing up to the gym while getting honest numbers about calorie intake.

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • Zero friction beats perfect UI — text-based logging in 10 seconds wins over 11-step app workflows every time.
  • Meal planning uses six context dimensions: fridge contents, restrictions, budget, schedule, household, and cooking time.
  • Meal plans flow into shopping lists and budget tracking — three chapters working as one integrated system.
  • Fitness tracking remembers your history, injuries, and goals — it asks "how's the knee?" not just "how far did you run?"
  • Medication reminders with confirmation, follow-ups, and escalation are genuinely important — missed doses have real consequences.
  • Choose your coach persona: encouraging, data-driven, or a mix. The agent's tone shapes whether you stick with tracking.
  • Your agent is NOT medical advice — it tracks and reminds, but never diagnoses or prescribes.
  • Health data is the most sensitive category — use local LLMs, encrypted storage, and never connect to insurance or employer systems.
