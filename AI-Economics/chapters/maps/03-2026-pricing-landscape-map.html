The Big Idea

Every AI provider's price sheet has seven knobs you should learn to read in under a minute. The cheapest credible model for a task is almost always cheaper than your instinct — and standing still on last year's model choice is one of the most expensive mistakes in AI cost engineering.

💵 The $/1M Mental Model

📏
Always per-million
Every provider prices per 1M tokens in 2026. Think in that unit; translate to monthly bills in your head.
🧮
Four price brackets
Nano ($0.20–$0.50/M in), Mini ($0.50–$1/M), Standard ($1.25–$3/M), Premium ($2–$30/M).
🎯
Cheapest credible model
Start at the cheapest tier that could work. Evaluate up only if the eval fails. Never start at the top.
📉
Re-shop yearly
Your model choice from 12 months ago is probably 50–90% more expensive than the best current option.

🔍 The 7 Things to Check on Any Price Sheet

  1. Input rate per 1M

    The sticker price. First number to grab.

  2. Output rate per 1M

    Usually 4–8x input. The real cost driver on most features.

  3. Cache read & write multipliers

    Read the actual rate, not just the "up to X% off" banner.

  4. Batch API discount

    Flat 50% across all three major providers in April 2026.

  5. Context-size surcharge

    Gemini Pro ≥200K = 2x. GPT-5.4 ≥272K = 2x input, 1.5x output. Anthropic: none.

  6. Rate-limit tier structure

    How much RPM/TPM your spend tier actually gets. (Ch. 8 deep-dives.)

  7. Reasoning-token billing

    Billed as output on Anthropic and OpenAI. Check for extended-thinking flags.

🏢 Provider-Specific Quirks

🟠
Anthropic: 1M flat rate
No surcharge at huge context sizes. Cache write 1.25x (5m) or 2x (1h); read 0.1x.
🟢
OpenAI: auto prefix cache
10% of input on cached prefix — no markers needed. Put dynamic content at the end.
🔵
Gemini: 200K tier
Pro input/output roughly doubles above 200K. Flash tiers have no surcharge.
🆓
Free tiers shifting
Gemini Pro went paid-only April 1, 2026. Anthropic moved enterprise to fully usage-based.

📖 Key Terms

Per-1M pricing
The universal unit in 2026. Every provider quotes input and output rates per 1M tokens.
Context-size surcharge
Higher rate above a threshold. Gemini Pro: 200K. GPT-5.4: 272K. Anthropic: none.
Cache-write multiplier
The premium paid the first time a prefix is cached. Anthropic 1.25x (5m) / 2x (1h).
Cache-read discount
0.1x input on all three providers — a ~90% discount on repeat-prefix calls.
Cheapest credible model
The lowest-priced model that passes your eval. The default starting point for new features.
Pricing sentinel
Quarterly-rotating role on the team: one engineer re-verifies rates against live pricing pages.
Yearly model re-shop
Audit of existing features against current prices. Typical annual saving: 50–90%.

🧠 Quick Quiz

Which major provider doesn't charge extra for huge context?

Anthropic. A 900K-token request on Sonnet 4.6 or Opus 4.7 pays the same per-token rate as a 9K one.

What's the "cheapest credible model" rule?

Start at the lowest-priced model that could plausibly do the task. Evaluate with your eval set. Only escalate tier-by-tier if quality fails. Never start at Opus or GPT-5.4 Pro by default.

Above what context size does GPT-5.4's per-session pricing double?

272K input tokens. Above that, GPT-5.4 and GPT-5.4 Pro charge 2x input and 1.5x output for the full session.

What's the three-column check before switching provider?

Cost per call, quality on your eval set, and p95 latency on real prompts. Cheaper provider must pass all three — not just cost.

When did Gemini's Pro free tier end?

April 1, 2026. Gemini Pro models are now paid-only; Flash and Flash-Lite still have limited free tiers.

Key Takeaways

  • Read every price sheet against the same 7-item checklist.
  • Always think in $/1M tokens — it's the universal unit in 2026.
  • Start new features at the cheapest credible tier, not the premium one.
  • Re-shop every running feature once a year — the best choice changes annually.
  • Cache rates, context thresholds, and batch discounts are often bigger levers than the sticker price.
  • Don't forget the side-bills: embeddings, vision, fine-tune surcharges, hosted tool calls.
  • Cheapest on paper ≠ cheapest in reality — check quality and latency too.
  • Keep a dated pricing.2026-QN.md in your repo. Rotate a pricing sentinel quarterly.
