Chapter 11

Fine-Tuning ROI: When Custom Pays Off

Memory Map
🧵

The Big Idea

Fine-tuning is like having a bespoke suit tailored for your body. Amazing fit when your body doesn’t change. Expensive waste when your product pivots next month. The training bill is a rounding error — data prep + eval is where the real money goes.

🧪 Distill-from-Opus pattern

  1. Generate synthetic data with Opus

    5K–50K input/output pairs for your narrow task. Cost: ~$25–$100.

  2. LoRA fine-tune a small OSS model

    Llama 4 Scout or Mistral Small 4. 4× H100 × 4 hrs ≈ $43.

  3. Serve the fine-tune self-hosted

    ~$1/1M tokens vs $5–$25/1M on Opus. 90–96% cheaper.

  4. Payback

    At 10M ticket classifications/mo: payback in < 1 day, $9,400/mo saved forever.

📖 Key terms

LoRA
Low-Rank Adaptation — train small adapter matrices on top of a frozen base model. Achieves ~95% of full-tune quality at ~10% of the cost.
QLoRA
Quantized LoRA — base model in 4-bit + LoRA adapters. Runs on consumer GPUs like an RTX 4090.
Full fine-tune
Retrain every parameter. Expensive, risky; rarely the right choice in 2026.
Distillation
Use a big model to generate training data, then fine-tune a small model on that data. Transfers capability cheaply.
Provisioned Throughput
AWS Bedrock’s dedicated-capacity billing for custom Claude 3 Haiku. Has a flat monthly floor.
Eval suite
200–500 labeled examples + scoring function that returns a single number. The prerequisite for any fine-tune.
RAG
Retrieval-Augmented Generation — inject relevant docs at inference time. The “tailored” rung of the ladder.
Adapter
A small set of extra weights trained on top of a frozen base. Multiple adapters can share a single GPU.

⚠️ Classic mistakes

🚫
FT before PMF
Product pivots; dataset becomes worthless; repeat forever.
🎲
No eval suite
Can’t tell if training helped. Vibes-based sign-off ships regressions.
🧠
FT for reasoning
Wrong tool. For deeper reasoning, buy a better base model.
🏚️
Forgetting serving cost
$500–$3K/mo dedicated-endpoint fixed floor destroys the ROI.

🧪 Quick quiz

Which rung of the ladder should you try first?

Prompt engineering. It’s free and often enough. Only climb if it fails.

LoRA vs full fine-tune — rule of thumb?

LoRA ≈ 95% of the quality at ~10% of the cost. Default to LoRA.

What’s the biggest line item in a fine-tune budget?

Engineering — data prep + eval iteration — not training compute. Training is usually under 5% of total.

Can you fine-tune Claude natively on Anthropic?

No. In April 2026, only Claude 3 Haiku via AWS Bedrock (US West — Oregon), and inference requires Provisioned Throughput.

What does “distill-from-Opus” mean?

Use Opus (or any frontier model) to generate synthetic training data, then fine-tune a small open model on it. Transfers ~95% of the capability for a narrow task at small-model cost forever.

🎯 If you remember nothing else

  • 🪜 Prompt → RAG → fine-tune, in that order. Don’t skip rungs.
  • 🚫 Never fine-tune before product-market fit.
  • 🪶 LoRA hits ~95% of full-tune quality at ~10% of the cost — default.
  • 💸 Training compute is a rounding error; data + eval is 80% of cost.
  • 📏 No eval suite, no fine-tune. 200+ labeled examples, scored in < 10 min.
  • 🧬 Distill-from-Opus: 94%+ savings on narrow tasks, < 1 day payback.
  • 🧠 Don’t fine-tune for reasoning — buy a better base model.
  • 🏢 Native Claude FT is Bedrock-only (Haiku) in April 2026.
  • 🏚️ Budget the serving floor, not just the training bill.
