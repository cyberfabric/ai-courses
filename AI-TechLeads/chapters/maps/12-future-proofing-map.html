The Big Idea

Future-proofing is like earthquake-proofing a building — you can't predict when the next quake hits, but you can build flexible foundations that absorb the shock. Build anti-fragile processes that get stronger with change, not weaker. Invest 70% in timeless skills, 30% in current tools.

🚀 The Agentic Evolution

  1. Autocomplete

    AI suggests the next line. Human drives everything.

  2. Chat + Edit

    AI generates multi-file changes from descriptions. Human reviews & merges.

  3. Agent Mode

    AI executes multi-step tasks: writes code, runs tests, fixes errors. Human sets the goal.

  4. Autonomous Agents

    AI runs for hours, builds features end-to-end. Human provides strategy & reviews output.

From 0% to 92% AI adoption in 36 months — not evolution, revolution

Haiku for speed, Sonnet for daily work, Opus for complexity, specialized for specific tasks. A model stack, not a model choice.

Beyond code generation: AI in planning, review, testing, deployment, and monitoring. Biggest wins often aren't in coding.

🎪 Hype Cycle Evaluation

  1. Does it solve a REAL problem?

    Not hypothetical — one your team complained about this quarter.

  2. Can we test in under 2 weeks?

    Good tools prove value quickly. 3-month evaluations = too much risk.

  3. Works with existing workflow?

    "Throw everything out" tools rarely succeed.

  4. 6-month survival rate?

    Well-funded? Production users? Active community (not just hype)?

  5. What's the exit strategy?

    If it disappears tomorrow, how hard is migration?

Quick Quiz

What's the key shift from copilots to agents?

Developers move from writing code to coordinating agents, defining problems precisely, designing guardrails, and reviewing autonomous output. The role shifts from implementation to strategic oversight.

Why invest 70% in timeless skills vs 30% in current tools?

Tools change every 6–12 months, but architecture, problem decomposition, and judgment compound over a career. Teams with strong fundamentals adapt to new tools quickly.

What makes an engineering org "anti-fragile"?

Tool-agnostic processes, rapid evaluation capability (2 weeks not 6 months), decision documentation, vendor independence (<50% with one vendor), learning culture, and psychological safety.

Should you fine-tune a model on your codebase?

Probably not yet. Context engineering (CLAUDE.md, .cursorrules, ADRs) delivers 80% of the benefit at 5% of the cost. Exhaust context engineering first; fine-tune only when that's not enough.

What are the 5 questions to evaluate AI hype?

1) Solves a real problem? 2) Testable in 2 weeks? 3) Works with existing workflow? 4) 6-month survival rate? 5) Exit strategy if it disappears?

Key Takeaways

  • AI is evolving from autocomplete (2023) to autonomous agents (2026) — prepare by investing in problem decomposition, context files, and test suites.
  • Five trends: agentic development, AI-native environments, multi-model strategies, custom fine-tuning, and AI across the full SDLC.
  • Timeless skills (architecture, judgment, communication) matter MORE as AI improves, not less. 70/30 learning split.
  • Build anti-fragile orgs: tool-agnostic processes, rapid evaluation, vendor independence, psychological safety.
  • Evaluate hype with the 5-question framework and 2-week test. Most new tools are "meh" — and that's valuable information.
  • Follow the 12-month roadmap: Foundation → Optimize → Scale → Evolve.
  • The course in one sentence: AI is a leadership challenge, not a technology one.
