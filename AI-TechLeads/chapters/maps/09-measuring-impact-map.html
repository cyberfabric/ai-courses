Chapter 9

Measuring AI Impact on Productivity

Memory Map
💡

The Big Idea

Most teams measure AI productivity wrong. Track outcomes across 4 dimensions (Speed, Quality, Developer Experience, Business) — not vanity metrics like lines of code or PR count. AI doesn't fix a team; it amplifies what's already there.

📊 The AI Productivity Paradox

🚀 Individual output up 21%
→
📈 98% more PRs merged
→
📉 Org delivery stays flat
→
🔄 Rework & review absorb gains

Source: Faros AI telemetry, 10,000+ developers (2025)

🚫 Vanity Metrics to Avoid

📝
Lines of Code
AI generates verbose code. More lines ≠ more value.
🔢
PR Count
Tiny PRs inflate count without shipping features.
⚡
Commit Frequency
Measures keyboard activity, not business value.
✅
AI Acceptance Rate
44% average — accepting more ≠ better code.
⏱️
Self-Reported Time Saved
METR trial: devs thought +20% faster, actually 19% slower.
👤
Individual Dev Speed
Creates perverse incentives. Measure team outcomes instead.

Key finding: 25% more AI adoption → 1.5% less throughput, 7.2% less stability (DORA 2025)

📏 Metrics by Dimension

🏎️
Speed
Cycle time • Time-to-first-PR • Time-in-review • Rework rate. Benchmarks: 30–40% cycle time reduction, 3–5 hrs saved/week.
🛡️
Quality
Defect escape rate • Production incidents • Code coverage trends • Architectural conformance • AI code churn (2x human baseline).
😊
Developer Experience
Tool adoption rate (>50% in 90 days) • DevEx survey • Frustration signals • Focus time. Power user density target: >40%.
💰
Business Outcomes
Features per sprint • Customer-reported bugs • Time-to-market • Developer cost per feature.

⚖️ The Comparison Problem

ApproachVerdictWhy
A/B test teams (half get AI, half don't) ❌ Don't do this Demoralizes control group, devs quit, data meaningless
Before/after (same team) ✅ Recommended Controls for team capability, uses historical data
Task-type comparison ✅ Good supplement AI helps more on greenfield than infrastructure work
Cross-team benchmarking ⚠️ Use carefully Watch for confounders (size, domain, experience)

📋 Dashboard Setup Steps

  1. Pick 5–7 Metrics

    At least 1 from each SQDB dimension. Cover speed, quality, DX, and business.

  2. Establish Baselines

    Mine historical data or start fresh. Even rough baselines beat none.

  3. Set Targets & Thresholds

    Red/yellow/green for 3, 6, and 12-month horizons.

  4. Assign Owners

    Every metric needs someone responsible for updating and acting on it.

  5. Monthly Review

    15 minutes in your existing team meeting. One-page exec summary.

  6. Quarterly Reassessment

    Are you measuring the right things? Drop what doesn't inform decisions.

📖 Key Terms

AI Productivity Paradox
Individual output rises but organizational delivery stays flat — rework and review absorb speed gains.
SQDB Framework
Speed, Quality, Developer Experience, Business Outcomes — the 4 dimensions of real AI impact.
Vanity Metrics
Easy-to-track numbers (LoC, PR count, commits) that always go up with AI but don't reflect real value.
DORA Metrics
Deployment Frequency, Lead Time, Change Failure Rate, MTTR — delivery performance gold standard.
AI Code Churn
Percentage of AI-generated code rewritten within 30 days — benchmarked at ~2x human code churn.
Power User Density
% of developers using AI tools daily across multiple features — target >40% for real adoption.
60% Utilization Discount
Not all "saved time" converts to productive output — apply this factor to avoid overstating ROI.

Quick Quiz

Why is "lines of code" a vanity metric for AI productivity?

AI generates verbose code — more lines often means more bloat, not more value. It incentivizes quantity over quality.

What are the 4 dimensions of the SQDB framework?

Speed, Quality, Developer Experience, and Business Outcomes. Healthy AI adoption improves at least 3 of 4.

Why shouldn't you A/B test teams (AI vs no AI)?

The control group feels "punished" — morale drops, devs quit, and the comparison becomes meaningless. Use before/after on the same team instead.

What's a healthy ROI range for AI coding tools?

2.5–3.5x average, 4–6x top quartile. If your calculation shows 10x+, you're probably undercounting costs or overcounting benefits.

Why does the DORA report say AI adoption correlates with reduced delivery stability?

Not because AI is bad, but because teams haven't adapted their processes. AI amplifies what's already there — including process weaknesses.

Key Takeaways

  • Most teams measure AI wrong — vanity metrics (LoC, PRs, commits) always go up but don't reflect real value.
  • The AI Productivity Paradox: individual output rises 21% while org delivery stays flat.
  • Use the SQDB Framework: Speed, Quality, Developer Experience, Business Outcomes — at least 3 of 4 should improve.
  • DORA metrics are your foundation, but AI needs additional metrics on top (adoption, churn, DX).
  • Never A/B test teams — use before/after, task-type comparison, or cross-team benchmarking.
  • Healthy AI tool ROI is 2.5–3.5x (average) — apply a 60% utilization discount to avoid the 20–40% overstatement trap.
  • The analogy: measuring AI is like measuring a new IDE — the tool matters less than how well the team adapts their workflow around it.
