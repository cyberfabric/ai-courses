Chapter 14

Background-Work Restrictions on Modern Android

Memory Map
💡

The Big Idea

Modern Android (15 / 16) restricts background work in two distinct ways: noisy exceptions like ForegroundServiceStartNotAllowedException from a BOOT_COMPLETED receiver, and silent failures where jobs simply don’t fire because of standby buckets, the 6-hour FGS timeout, or Android 16 quota concurrency. The same diagnostic loop — signature → AI → root cause → skill — works for both, you just need to know where to look.

📅 The Restriction Timeline

  1. Android 12

    Background apps can no longer start FGS in most cases. ForegroundServiceStartNotAllowedException is born.

  2. Android 13

    POST_NOTIFICATIONS runtime permission. Users can revoke FGS notifications — FGS still runs but is silent.

  3. Android 14

    FGS must declare a foregroundServiceType. New shortService type. Microphone restricted from BOOT_COMPLETED.

  4. Android 15

    BOOT_COMPLETED restrictions extended to most types. 6-hour dataSync & mediaProcessing timeouts. New mediaProcessing type.

  5. Android 16

    JobScheduler quotas now enforced concurrent with FGS and in active bucket. setImportantWhileForeground() ignored. New getPendingJobReasonsHistory API.

🚨 The Three Signatures You Must Know

🔌
BOOT_COMPLETED FGS Crash
ForegroundServiceStartNotAllowedException with message BG-FGS-start not allowed: BOOT_COMPLETED restricted for type <type>. Fires the moment a user reboots after your release.
⏱️
6-Hour FGS Timeout
RemoteServiceException: "did not stop within its timeout" if you don’t handle onTimeout(). Or "Time limit already exhausted for foreground service type dataSync" when starting.
🔇
Silent Job Skip (Android 16)
No exception. Just a job that doesn’t run. Look in dumpsys jobscheduler for STOP_REASON_QUOTA, STOP_REASON_DEVICE_STATE, or STOP_REASON_TIMEOUT.

🛠️ The ADB Cheat Sheet

Force BOOT_COMPLETED restriction
adb shell am compat enable FGS_BOOT_COMPLETED_RESTRICTIONS <pkg> — reproduce the crash on any Android 15+ device without bumping targetSdk.
Force the 6-hour timeout
adb shell am compat enable FGS_INTRODUCE_TIME_LIMITS <pkg>
Shrink the timeout for testing
adb shell device_config put activity_manager data_sync_fgs_timeout_duration 60000 — one-minute timeout instead of six hours.
Inspect job state
adb shell dumpsys jobscheduler | grep -A 30 <pkg> — look for STOP_REASON_*.
Check standby bucket
adb shell am get-standby-bucket <pkg> → active | working_set | frequent | rare | restricted.
Force standby bucket
adb shell am set-standby-bucket <pkg> active for testing without throttling.
Capture for Battery Historian
adb bugreport > bug.zip, then upload to Battery Historian web UI.

🆕 Android 16: The New Diagnostic APIs

🔍
getPendingJobReasons(jobId)
Returns an int[] of PENDING_JOB_REASON_* constants — finally tells you why a job hasn’t fired right now.
📜
getPendingJobReasonsHistory(jobId)
Recent constraint changes — great for “why was it pending five minutes ago?” investigations.
🪦
setImportantWhileForeground
Silently ignored in Android 16. Drop the call — it doesn’t throw, it just does nothing now.
⚖️
Concurrent FGS no longer exempt
Pre-16 loophole closed: jobs running concurrent with an FGS now adhere to their runtime quota.

🧠 Key Terms

FGS (Foreground Service)
A service the user is meant to be aware of via a notification. Runs at higher priority but with stricter rules than ever in 2026.
foregroundServiceType
Required attribute on <service> in the manifest since Android 14. Determines which restrictions apply.
BG-FGS-start
The OS’s shorthand for “starting an FGS while the app is in background.” Restricted since Android 12, much more restricted in 15+.
App Standby Bucket
active → working_set → frequent → rare → restricted. Lower buckets get less background runtime.
STOP_REASON_QUOTA
JobScheduler stop reason indicating the app exceeded its job runtime quota for the bucket.
onTimeout(int, int)
Service callback (Android 15+) signaling the FGS’s 6-hour budget is up. You have a few seconds to call stopSelf().
WorkManager
The 2026 default for non-user-visible background work. Handles retries, constraints, batching, and survives every restriction in this chapter.

🧪 Quick Quiz

Your release added targetSdk=35. Three weeks later, support tickets say “sync stopped after reboot.” What restriction probably fired?

BOOT_COMPLETED FGS restriction. Most types (dataSync, camera, mediaPlayback, mediaProjection, phoneCall, microphone, mediaProcessing) can no longer be started from a BOOT_COMPLETED receiver on Android 15+. Migrate to WorkManager.

You implement Service.onTimeout() but get killed by RemoteServiceException anyway. What went wrong?

You took longer than a few seconds to call stopSelf(). The OS gives you only a brief grace period after onTimeout() — persist the bare-minimum checkpoint and stop. Resume work via WorkManager later.

How do you reproduce the 6-hour timeout in test without waiting six hours?

adb shell am compat enable FGS_INTRODUCE_TIME_LIMITS <pkg> to force the behavior, then adb shell device_config put activity_manager data_sync_fgs_timeout_duration 60000 to drop the window to one minute.

A job runs fine on Android 15 but stops mid-execution and re-schedules on Android 16. The app has an FGS running. What changed?

Android 16 closed the “FGS exempts your jobs from their quota” loophole. Jobs concurrent with an FGS now adhere to their runtime quota. Use JobScheduler#getPendingJobReasonsHistory(jobId) to confirm.

Where do you look to find out why a job is silently pending?

Three places: adb shell dumpsys jobscheduler | grep -A 30 <pkg> (look for STOP_REASON_*), adb shell am get-standby-bucket <pkg> (you might be in rare or restricted), and Battery Historian on a recent bug report (visualize the FGS and JobScheduler swimlanes).

Key Takeaways

  • When a release bumps targetSdk, ask first: which OS-version restrictions just turned on for me? Crashes weeks later usually trace back to that single flip.
  • BOOT_COMPLETED can no longer legally start most FGS types on Android 15+. The fix is almost always WorkManager, not a different FGS type.
  • The 6-hour dataSync / mediaProcessing ceiling is real. Implement Service.onTimeout(int, int), persist a checkpoint within seconds, call stopSelf().
  • Android 16 closed the “jobs are exempt while an FGS is running” loophole. setImportantWhileForeground() is silently ignored. Use getPendingJobReasonsHistory() to debug.
  • For silent failures, the diagnostic stack is: reproduce on a 15/16 device → dumpsys jobscheduler → Battery Historian → standby-bucket sanity check.
  • You can force every restriction on without bumping targetSdk via adb shell am compat enable FGS_BOOT_COMPLETED_RESTRICTIONS and FGS_INTRODUCE_TIME_LIMITS. No excuse to wait for production.
  • Add /diagnose-background-restriction to your team’s commands. The taxonomy is finite; the skill makes triage minutes instead of days.
