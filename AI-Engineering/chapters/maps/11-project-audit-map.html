Chapter 11

The AI-Ready Audit: Your Project Scorecard

Memory Map

💡 The Big Idea

📋

Measure, Then Improve

Score your project across all 5 pillars (0–4 each, 20 max). Fix the lowest-scoring pillar first — a zero in any pillar undermines all the others. A project with 2-2-2-2-2 outperforms one with 4-4-4-4-0.

🏛️ The 5 Pillars

📄
P1: CLAUDE.md
Project identity, NEVER rules, build commands, naming conventions, multi-level hierarchy.
🧠
P2: Context
.claude/rules/ files, ARCHITECTURE.md, dual-format docs, load-on-demand pattern, context budget.
⚡
P3: Skills & Hooks
Team skill library in .claude/skills/<name>/SKILL.md (legacy .claude/commands/ still works), safety hooks, quality hooks, plan-execute-validate skills.
📐
P4: Architecture Rules
MUST/SHOULD/PREFER severity, rule IDs, 10 topic areas, compliance checking.
👥
P5: Team Config
Shared setup, onboarding playbook, adoption metrics, MCP config, skill contributions.

📊 Scoring Scale (per pillar)

ScoreLevelWhat It Looks Like
0AbsentNothing exists. AI has zero project-specific guidance.
1MinimalSomething exists but it's generic or personal-only.
2FunctionalSpecific and shared. AI produces mostly-correct output.
3ComprehensiveFull coverage. Team follows consistently. Maintenance cadence.
4ExcellentEvolved continuously. Team contributes. Metrics tracked.

🚫 Common Audit Mistakes

🎮
Gaming the Score
Checking boxes without real depth. A genuine 14 that knows its gaps beats an inflated 18.
🏃
Fixing Everything at Once
Spreading effort across all 5 pillars simultaneously. Focus on lowest pillar first.
📸
One-Time Snapshot
Running the audit once and never again. Configuration drifts — audit quarterly.
🧍
Solo Audit
One person scores alone. Run it as a team exercise — the disagreements reveal real gaps.

❓ Quick Quiz

Test Yourself

What are the 5 pillars of the audit?

P1: CLAUDE.md & Project Identity. P2: Context Engineering. P3: Skills & Hooks. P4: Architecture Rules. P5: Team Configuration. Each scored 0–4, max total 20.

Why should you fix the lowest pillar first?

Because a zero in any pillar creates a bottleneck that undermines all the others. A project scoring 4-4-4-4-0 performs worse than 2-2-2-2-2. Balance is more important than depth in one area.

What score makes a project "AI-Ready"?

15–17 out of 20 = AI-Ready. Strong configuration across all pillars. AI is a reliable team member. Focus shifts to maintaining and tuning.

How often should you re-run the audit?

Quarterly. Add /audit-readiness to your quarterly planning. Configuration drifts as the codebase evolves — regular audits catch gaps before they become problems.

What's the fastest way from 0 to 10?

The one-week quick wins: Day 1 CLAUDE.md, Day 2 .claude/rules/ files, Day 3 starter skills, Day 4 hooks, Day 5 team demo. Gets every pillar to at least score 2 (= total 10).

📌 Key Takeaways

If You Remember Nothing Else

  • 5 pillars, scored 0–4 each. CLAUDE.md, Context, Skills/Hooks, Architecture Rules, Team Config. Max score: 20.
  • Fix the lowest pillar first. A zero anywhere is a bottleneck. Balance (2-2-2-2-2) beats lopsided excellence (4-4-4-4-0).
  • 15+ = AI-Ready. Below 10 = significant gaps. 18+ = AI-Native. Know where you are to plan where you're going.
  • One-week quick wins can take any project from 0 to 10: CLAUDE.md, .claude/rules/ files, 2 skills (in .claude/skills/), safety hooks, team demo.
  • Quarter-long transformation: Month 1 foundation (10+), Month 2 depth (15+), Month 3 maturity (18+).
  • Audit quarterly. Use /audit-readiness to automate. Configuration drifts — regular checks keep it honest.
