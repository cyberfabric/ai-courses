The Big Idea

An AI-ready project teaches the AI your architecture, conventions, and forbidden patterns before you type a single prompt. The difference between 30% and 95% AI success rate isn't better prompting — it's better project configuration.

🏛️ The 5 Pillars of AI Readiness

📋
Pillar 1: CLAUDE.md
Your project's AI brain — rules, constraints, architecture decisions. The blueprint of the house.
📦
Pillar 2: Context Engineering
What AI reads, how much, in what format. Choosing which materials to bring to the job site.
🔧
Pillar 3: Skills & Hooks
Reusable commands and automated quality gates. The power tools of AI engineering.
📐
Pillar 4: Architecture Rules
Encoded standards in dual format (checklists + .ctx files). The building codes.
👥
Pillar 5: Team Configuration
Shared setup, onboarding, and maintenance processes. Every worker reads the same blueprint.

🧪 The New Developer Test

  1. Clone the Repo

    New developer gets the project.

  2. Open Claude Code

    No prior briefing needed.

  3. Ask for a Feature

    "Add a new screen that shows favorite recipes."

  4. Evaluate the Output

    Pass: Correct architecture, patterns, module placement. Fail: Developer has to explain conventions or correct patterns.

If your project fails this test within 30 minutes, your AI configuration isn't specific enough.

🍳 Recipes App: The Configuration Stack

📋
CLAUDE.md

📦
Context
10 architecture rule files in dual format + ARCHITECTURE.md + module READMEs.

Skills & Hooks

📐
Architecture Rules
10 topic areas × MUST/SHOULD/PREFER severity + .ctx condensed versions.
👥
Team Config
Onboarding checklist, shared MCP config, skill review, monthly CLAUDE.md review.

🔑 The CLAUDE.md Insight

📝

Encode Decisions, Not Knowledge

AI already knows Clean Architecture. What it doesn't know is that your project uses StateFlow (not LiveData), single-Activity navigation, and max 3 public functions per ViewModel. Encode your specific decisions.

🧪 Quick Quiz

What's the difference between AI-Assisted and AI-Configured?

AI-Assisted has a basic CLAUDE.md and maybe a skill or two (~55% success). AI-Configured has detailed CLAUDE.md, architecture rules, skills library, and hooks (~80% success).

What are the 5 pillars of AI readiness?

1) CLAUDE.md, 2) Context Engineering, 3) Skills & Hooks, 4) Architecture Rules, 5) Team Configuration.

Why is "use Clean Architecture" a bad CLAUDE.md rule?

It's too vague. AI already knows Clean Architecture — it needs your specific decisions: StateFlow vs LiveData, naming conventions, module boundaries, forbidden patterns.

What does the 30-minute test measure?

Whether a new developer can clone your repo, open Claude Code, and produce architecture-compliant code on their first prompt — without any human explanation.

How does the compounding effect work?

One rule encoded in 10 minutes saves 2 min/interaction × 5 devs × 10 requests/day = 100 min saved daily. Every future interaction benefits automatically.

📌 Key Takeaways

  • Your prompting isn't the problem — your project configuration is.
  • AI readiness rests on 5 pillars: CLAUDE.md, context, skills/hooks, architecture rules, and team config.
  • The gap between 30% and 95% AI success rate is configuration, not better models.
  • A good CLAUDE.md encodes decisions (StateFlow, not LiveData), not knowledge (what Clean Architecture is).
  • Every rule encoded once compounds across every future interaction for every team member.
  • The 30-minute new developer test is the ultimate measure of AI readiness.
  • Run the Quick Readiness Check, commit your baseline — you'll compare against it in Chapter 11.
