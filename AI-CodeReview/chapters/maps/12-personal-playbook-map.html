💡 The Big Idea

🏋️

Gym Membership vs. Actually Going

Knowledge without practice is just trivia. This course gave you the techniques — checklists, workflows, tools. This chapter is about showing up. Not at maximum intensity, but consistently, with a routine that fits your real life. The only wrong playbook is the one you abandon after three days.

☀️ Daily Habits

  1. 5-Minute Morning Scan

    git log --since="yesterday" — for each commit, ask: can I explain what this does and why? If not, flag for weekly deep-dive.

  2. 2× PR Review Blocks (30 min each)

    After standup + mid-afternoon. Block on calendar like a meeting. Can't skip.

  3. The "One Question" Rule

    For every PR reviewed, ask at least one substantive question about logic, intent, or edge cases. Not formatting nitpicks.

📆 Weekly Deep-Dive (60-90 min)

🔍
Deep-Dive One Module (30 min)
Pick one complex AI-generated module. Apply the 5-step comprehension workflow. Write a journal entry.
🚫
No-AI Coding (30 min)
Fix a bug, write tests, refactor — without AI. The session most people drop first. Also the most important to keep.
💬
Review AI Chat History (15 min)
What did you ask about most? Those topics are your knowledge gaps. Add to skills tracker.
📓
Update Learning Journal (15 min)
What did you learn? What surprised you? What would you do differently? Writing consolidates learning.

🔑 Key Terms

Morning Review
5-minute daily scan of yesterday's merged code. Early-warning system for comprehension gaps.
One Question Rule
Ask at least one substantive question per PR review. Forces engagement over rubber-stamping.
No-AI Coding Session
Weekly 30-minute manual coding session. Cross-training that maintains the skills AI can't replace.
Skills Audit
Monthly 15-minute self-assessment across 5 core skills. Declining skills become next month's focus.
Proactive + Reactive Loop
Feed context before AI writes (proactive) + review after it writes (reactive). Neither alone is sufficient.
Risk-Based Allocation
Match review depth to risk level. Full workflow for auth/payments, quick scan for config/styling.

❓ Quick Quiz

Test Your Understanding

What are the three rhythms of the review playbook?

Daily: 5-min morning scan + 2×30-min PR review blocks. Weekly: 60-90 min deep-dive (module comprehension + No-AI coding + chat review + journal). Monthly: 15-min skills audit comparing scores to last month.

Why is the No-AI coding session the most important habit to keep?

A developer who can't code without AI can't review AI's output, can't debug AI's mistakes, and can't evaluate whether AI's approach is correct. It's cross-training that maintains the skills that make you effective with AI.

What's the difference between proactive and reactive AI review?

Proactive: feed context before AI writes (CLAUDE.md, specs, project rules) so it generates better code. Reactive: review, comprehend, and verify after AI writes (everything in this course). The combination is what makes you effective.

How do you know when you've over-calibrated your review process?

When your review process takes more time than it saves. Signs: 90 min per PR, three SAST tools on every change, No-AI Day every day, 60% velocity drop. Recalibrate with risk-based allocation and automated tools for low-hanging fruit.

What four metrics should you track for review effectiveness?

1) Bugs caught in review vs. escaped to production, 2) Comprehension score (monthly self-assessment), 3) Review time per PR (efficient but never zero), 4) Knowledge gaps closed per month.

📌 Key Takeaways

If You Remember Nothing Else

  • Daily: 5-min morning scan + two 30-min PR review blocks. Ask one real question per PR.
  • Weekly: Deep-dive one module + 30 min No-AI coding. Don't skip the No-AI session.
  • Monthly: 15-min skills audit. Declining skills become next month's focus.
  • Match review depth to risk: full workflow for auth/payments, quick scan for config/styling.
  • Proactive context (10 min specs) saves reactive time (30 min review). Do both.
  • Track 4 metrics: bugs caught, comprehension score, review time, gaps closed.
  • Review discipline is career insurance. The role is shifting from implementer to orchestrator.
  • Start tomorrow. Not Monday. Commit to two weeks. Keep what works, drop what doesn't.
