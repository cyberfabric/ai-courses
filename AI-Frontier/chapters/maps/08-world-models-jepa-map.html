Chapter 8

World Models & JEPA

Memory Map
🌍

The Big Idea

LLMs predict language about the world. World models predict the world itself. LeCun raised $1.03B for AMI Labs in March 2026 to bet on this distinction. JEPA, Genie 3, and NVIDIA Cosmos all qualify as “world models” — but they predict different things in different spaces. The 2026 reality: world models complete LLMs, they don’t replace them. The “ChatGPT moment” for world models still hasn’t arrived.

🧠 LeCun’s Critique of LLMs

🚫
No causal model
An LLM can describe gravity but cannot predict the ball will fall — only retrieve descriptions of falling balls.
🔁
No action loop
“If I rotate the cup 30 degrees, what does it look like?” Confident answers, frequent errors.
🎲
Generative noise
Pixel/token prediction forces the model to commit to details (exact shade, exact word) that aren’t actually predictable.
💰
$1B bet on the alternative
AMI Labs (LeCun’s post-Meta venture): $1.03B seed at $3.5B valuation, March 10, 2026. Built on JEPA.

The trick: predict in embedding space, not pixel/token space. The model commits only to what is actually predictable (motion, trajectory, broad pattern), not to noise (exact leaf positions).

Representation collapse. Without care, both encoders output the same constant vector (loss = 0, learned nothing). Fixed via EMA target encoder — the same fix BYOL/MoCo use. JEPA inherits this: hard to debug, no human-readable output, must probe via downstream tasks. A real reason the “LLM crowd” stays skeptical even when JEPA wins benchmarks.

📜 The JEPA Family Timeline

  1. I-JEPA — 2023 (images)

    First proof: predict embeddings of masked image patches. Competitive with MAE/BEiT at lower compute.

  2. V-JEPA — 2024 (video)

    Self-supervised video understanding without pixel reconstruction.

  3. V-JEPA 2 — June 2025 (video + action)

    1M+ hours of YouTube. 77.3% top-1 on Something-Something v2. Robot pick-and-place 65–80% on novel objects. Open-weight, commercial use.

  4. LLM-JEPA — September 2025

    JEPA principle applied to text: predict embeddings of masked spans, not tokens.

  5. VL-JEPA — December 2025

    1.6B params. Beats CLIP / SigLIP2 / Perception Encoder on 16 video benchmarks. 65.7% on WorldPrediction-WM — beats GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Gemini 2.0. Half the trainable params, 2.85× faster decoding.

📚 Key Terms

JEPA
Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture. Predict embeddings of masked content, not pixels/tokens.
World model
A learned simulator of how the world evolves — can be in embedding space, pixel space, or physics-grounded.
Context encoder
The JEPA component that embeds the visible (un-masked) input.
Target encoder
The JEPA component that embeds the masked (true) part. Updated as EMA of context encoder to prevent collapse.
Representation collapse
Failure mode where both encoders output the same constant vector. Loss = 0, learned nothing.
EMA target
Exponential moving average of context encoder weights. Standard fix for representation collapse (also used in BYOL, MoCo).
Physical AI
NVIDIA’s term for robotics + autonomous vehicles + simulation — the world-model market.
WorldPrediction-WM
Benchmark for predicting future world states. VL-JEPA at 65.7% beats GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Gemini 2.0.
AMI Labs
LeCun’s post-Meta venture, JEPA-based, $1.03B seed at $3.5B valuation, March 2026.
Genie 3 / Project Genie
DeepMind text-to-3D interactive world model, public Jan 29, 2026, gated behind AI Ultra ($250/mo) tier.

❓ Quick Quiz

Self-Test

Why does JEPA predict in embedding space instead of pixel/token space?

Most pixel-level details are genuinely unpredictable (exact leaf motion, exact cloud pattern). A pixel-loss penalizes the model for not predicting the unpredictable, so it averages and produces blurry output. Embedding space has compressed away the noise — the model only commits to what’s actually predictable.

What is representation collapse and how is it fixed?

Both encoders learn to output the same constant vector — loss is zero but the model learned nothing. The fix is an EMA (exponential moving average) target encoder that updates slowly while the context encoder is updated by gradients. Same trick BYOL and MoCo use.

How can a 1.6B-parameter VL-JEPA beat GPT-4o on WorldPrediction-WM?

Because the benchmark is about predicting future world states, which is the task JEPA was designed for. GPT-4o is large but trained on language about the world; VL-JEPA is trained to predict the world itself. On generic chat, GPT-4o still wins easily — the strengths don’t overlap.

Why is Genie 3’s “several minutes of consistency” a real breakthrough?

Earlier interactive world models (Genie 2, Sora-class) produced impressive single shots that fell apart on extended interaction. Persistence — the wall is still there when you turn around — is what makes Genie 3 a candidate building block for training environments and game worlds.

Why is NVIDIA giving Cosmos away under an open model license?

CUDA playbook. Make the foundation free; sell the GPUs that run it. By open-sourcing Cosmos, NVIDIA makes every robotics company a customer of the inference workload — which runs best on NVIDIA hardware.

What’s the “ChatGPT moment” status for world models?

Hasn’t arrived. Genie 3 is closest (real product, real users) but $250/mo, US-only, 18+, with a few minutes of consistency. The world-model breakthrough may arrive bundled with humanoid robots in 2026 — or not until later.

Key Takeaways

  • LLMs model language about the world; world models model the world itself. Different jobs, often complementary.
  • JEPA predicts in embedding space, not pixel/token space — commits only to what’s actually predictable.
  • V-JEPA 2 (June 2025) trained on 1M+ hours of YouTube; 65–80% pick-and-place on novel objects with a real robot.
  • VL-JEPA (Dec 2025) at 1.6B params beats GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Gemini 2.0 on WorldPrediction-WM.
  • Genie 3 (DeepMind) generates text-to-3D worlds at 720p/24fps with several minutes of consistency — first real persistent interactive world model.
  • NVIDIA Cosmos is the open-source platform layer for physical AI; 1X, Skild AI, Waabi, Uber are early customers.
  • LeCun raised $1.03B for AMI Labs in March 2026 betting that LLMs are not the path to general intelligence.
  • Hybrid future: LLM as the language layer, world model as the simulator backbone — not replacement, layering.
  • Version numbers will rot. The patterns — embedding vs pixel prediction, persistence as the simulator threshold, world models as perception backbones — will last.
