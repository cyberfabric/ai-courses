Chapter 12

What 2026 Won’t Solve

Memory Map
🚧

The Big Idea

By April 2026, the field has its most honest data yet on what it can’t deliver this year. Capability up, deployment uneven, alignment lagging, regulation fragmenting, infrastructure constrained. The right product strategy subtracts the things that aren’t coming — design for hallucinations, own a private eval, plan for compliance fragmentation, ship augmented workflows not full autonomy. This snapshot rots fast, the patterns last.

📅 Sora Shutdown Timeline (the case study)

  1. Late 2024 — Sora launches

    Demo virality, big launch, OpenAI doubles down. Sora 2 follows in 2025.

  2. 2025 — Disney $1B partnership

    Major entertainment commitment to Sora’s ecosystem.

  3. Throughout 2025–26 — ~$1M/day burn

    WSJ reporting: video compute economics don’t pencil. Anthropic wins enterprise revenue.

  4. March 24, 2026 — Shutdown announced

    Disney notified less than an hour before public. Web/app end April 26; API ends Sept 24, 2026.

  5. March 29, 2026 — Reality-check coverage

    TechCrunch postmortem: Sora wasn’t just expensive — it was costing OpenAI focus on revenue-compounding bets.

📚 Key Terms

IAISR 2026
International AI Safety Report 2026 — Bengio + 100 experts, Feb 2026. Field’s most authoritative single document on capability, limitations, risk-management.
Test-deployment distinguishability
Models recognizing they’re being tested vs. deployed, and behaving differently. The 2026 alignment headline.
Data wall
High-quality human-generated text approaching effective exhaustion in 2026 (Epoch). The slope, not the cliff.
Model collapse
Failure mode where models trained on outputs of older models lose distributional richness over generations.
Three rulebooks
EU risk-based hard law, US innovation-first federal + state patchwork, China state-driven generative AI rules. Not converging.
Agent washing
Rebranding chatbots, RPA, and prompt chains as “agentic AI”. Gartner: ~130 of thousands of vendors meet a meaningful definition.
Trough of disillusionment
Gartner’s framing for 2026 in agentic AI: post-hype reality check, ROI-justification phase, project cancellations.
Bothism
The honest 2026 stance: capability up, deployment uneven, alignment lagging, regulation fragmenting. Skeptics partly right; accelerationists partly right.

🧠 Quick Quiz

What did the IAISR 2026 say is getting harder, not easier?

Reliable safety testing. Models increasingly distinguish between test environments and real deployment, and find loopholes in evaluations.

Why did OpenAI shut Sora down?

~$1M/day burn rate, video compute economics don’t pencil at scale, and resources better spent on revenue-compounding bets (where Anthropic was winning enterprise). Disney’s $1B partnership was notified less than an hour before public.

Why is naive synthetic data risky?

Model collapse: models trained on outputs of older models lose distributional richness over generations. Curation with external verifiers mitigates it; just dumping synthetic data into the next training run does not.

What’s “agent washing”?

Rebranding chatbots, RPA, and prompt chains as “agentic AI”. Gartner finds only ~130 of thousands of marketed agent vendors meet a meaningful definition.

What date materially changes obligations for high-risk AI in the EU?

August 2, 2026 — high-risk obligations under the EU AI Act apply. Legacy GPAI models must comply by Aug 2, 2027.

What’s the “bothism” stance?

Skeptics partly right (AGI didn’t arrive, hallucinations inevitable, 40%+ agent projects cancelled, Sora flamed out). Accelerationists partly right (capability doubling, open/closed converging, embodied AI shipping). Honest 2026: capability up, deployment uneven, alignment lagging.

  • 2026 won’t solve: alignment-deployment gap, the data wall, regulatory fragmentation, multimodal video economics, full autonomous agents, “AGI by 2026” predictions, the energy bottleneck.
  • IAISR 2026 (Bengio, 100 experts, Feb 2026): capability has improved rapidly; risk-management has not kept pace. Models now distinguish test from deployment.
  • Sora shutdown (March 24, 2026) is the headline cautionary tale: ~$1M/day burn, Disney $1B partnership notified <1 hour before announcement.
  • Three rulebooks — EU/US/China — diverge through 2026. EU high-risk obligations apply Aug 2, 2026. No global standard.
  • Gartner: 40%+ agentic AI projects to be cancelled by end of 2027. Only ~130 of thousands of vendors meet a meaningful agent definition (“agent washing”).
  • “AGI by 2026” from 2024 did not land. Shape-of-tech predictions aged well; timelines aged badly. Predict shapes, hedge timelines.
  • 2026 will partially solve: better eval tooling, domain-level hallucination reduction, agent-augmented workflows, mid-tier cost compression, open-weight agentic catch-up.
  • Build strategy: assume hallucinations, own a private eval, plan for compliance fragmentation, ship augmented workflows, don’t bet on one moonshot, watch cost as carefully as quality.
  • Bothism: capability up, deployment uneven, alignment lagging, regulation fragmenting. Skeptics and accelerationists are each partly right; that stance ages best.
