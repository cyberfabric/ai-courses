🧬 The Five Durable Patterns

The named models will rotate. These shapes will not.

⚖️
1. Speed / Quality / Cost Triangle
Every lab now ships at least three tiers (fast/balanced/frontier). You pick the corner. The names rotate; the triangle doesn't.
🧠
2. Reasoning / Base Split
Each frontier lab ships both a fast base model and a slow "thinks out loud" reasoning variant. Different products, different failure modes.
🎨
3. Multimodal by Default
Text-only is now a downgrade. Every flagship handles text + image + audio (sometimes video) in one forward pass.
🧩
4. MoE Everywhere
DeepSeek V4: 49B active of 1.6T. GPT-4o, Gemini, Mixtral, Qwen, GLM, Kimi — all MoE. Dense flagships are the exception.
🪞
5. Leaderboard / Product Gap
Average ~37% gap between lab-reported eval and real-world deployment on agentic systems. Goodhart is permanent, not a 2026 bug.
💸
Bonus: Price Spread > 25×
Same nominal "frontier" task: $40 on GPT-5.4 vs $1.40 on hosted DeepSeek V4. Pick the corner that matches your task, not the headline.

📚 Key Terms

Frontier model
A production-grade language model at or near the current capability ceiling — in April 2026, that means Opus 4.7, GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1 Pro, Grok 4, Sonnet 5, DeepSeek V4.
SWE-Bench Verified
A real-world software-engineering benchmark of GitHub issue + patch pairs, hand-verified by humans. Sonnet 5 was the first model to break 80% (Feb 2026); Opus 4.7 hit 87.6% in April.
GPQA Diamond
Graduate-level science questions, "Google-proof." Frontier models cluster at 94.2–94.4% — functionally saturated.
Mixture-of-Experts (MoE)
An architecture where only a fraction of the model's parameters activate per token. DeepSeek V4-Pro: 1.6T total, 49B active. The economic unlock of the 2025–26 cycle.
Open weights
Parameters are downloadable; training data and pipeline usually aren't. Distinct from "open source" (everything public). DeepSeek V4 is open-weights, Apache 2.0.
Goodhart's Law
"When a measure becomes a target, it stops being a good measure." Why every saturated benchmark is partially broken.
Leaderboard / product gap
The systematic ~37% delta between lab-reported eval scores and real-world deployment performance on agentic systems.
Time-stamped course
This course's central trick: every chapter is dated, every claim cites a current source, and every chapter is honest about which parts will rot fastest.

🧪 Quick Quiz

Self-check

Which model was the first to break 80% on SWE-Bench Verified, and when?

Claude Sonnet 5 ("Fennec") on February 3, 2026, scoring 82.1%. Opus 4.7 followed in April at 87.6%.

Why are GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1 Pro, and Opus 4.7 all reporting ~94% on GPQA Diamond essentially meaningless as a comparison?

The benchmark is saturated. The three scores (94.4 / 94.3 / 94.2) are within the eval's noise band — they tell you all three are frontier-grade but tell you nothing about which is "best."

What's the difference between "open weights" and "open source" in 2026?

Open weights = the parameters are downloadable (you can run inference). Open source = also the training data, pipeline, and post-training recipe are public. DeepSeek V4 is open-weights but not strictly open-source.

What is the leaderboard / product gap, and why is it permanent?

It's the average ~37% delta between lab-reported eval scores and real-world deployment performance. It's permanent because of Goodhart's Law — whenever a benchmark becomes a target, vendors optimize against the measure and the measure loses validity.

Which "patterns" survive the next snapshot rotation, and which "facts" don't?

Survives: speed/quality/cost triangle, reasoning/base split, multimodal default, MoE everywhere, leaderboard/product gap. Rots: every version number, every benchmark score, every price, every leaderboard position.

🔑 Key Takeaways

  • Six models on the April 2026 frontier: Opus 4.7, GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1 Pro, Grok 4.20, Sonnet 5 ("Fennec"), and DeepSeek V4 (open weights).
  • Anthropic leads coding, OpenAI leads web research, Gemini leads multimodal & price, DeepSeek leads open-weights. No single "best."
  • Reasoning benchmarks (GPQA Diamond) have saturated — three top models within 0.2 pp.
  • Price spread is ~25× for the same nominal task. The strategic question is "saturated or differentiated?"
  • Leaderboards are theater. ~37% gap between eval and production. Run the five-step honesty filter before betting on a number.
  • Five patterns will outlast the version numbers: speed/quality/cost triangle, reasoning/base split, multimodal default, MoE everywhere, leaderboard/product gap.
  • Course mantra: this snapshot rots fast, the patterns last.
