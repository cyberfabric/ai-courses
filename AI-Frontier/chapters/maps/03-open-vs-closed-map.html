Chapter 3

Open vs Closed: The Race Tightens

Memory Map
🔓

The Big Idea

By April 2026, five Chinese labs simultaneously reached frontier quality on open weights. The race tightened — it didn’t end. Open beats closed on knowledge, math, and human preference. Closed still leads on production coding (especially private codebases) and long-horizon agentic work. And “open weights” is not “open source” — the training data and post-training recipes remain closed.

🔍 Why the Gap Closed

  1. Distillation got really good

    Synthetic data from frontier closed models, filtered for quality, became a viable training signal. You needed teachers, not raw data scale.

  2. MoE economics

    Activating 49B of 1.6T means inference cost roughly tracks the active count. Open labs could ship enormous models without Nvidia-scale inference budgets.

  3. Chinese national investment

    Compute, recruiting, infrastructure compounded together. Five labs hit the frontier simultaneously, not one at a time.

  4. Curated > raw scale

    Smaller, higher-quality, instruction-tuned datasets outperformed “more tokens.” Some frontier-level models trained on under 5T tokens.

  5. Open eval and rapid iteration

    Open community ships, evaluates, ships again on weekly cadence. Closed labs ship quarterly. Compounding rate is faster on the open side.

🎯 When to Choose Which

🔒
Choose open when…
Privacy / data residency, sovereignty, deep fine-tuning, >10M queries/day, regulated industries, long-term vendor independence.
☁️
Choose closed when…
Best agentic tasks, operational simplicity, sub-second SLAs, <1M queries/day, bleeding-edge capability, commercial indemnification.
🔀
Most teams: both
Closed flagship for the hardest tasks, open-weight for high-volume / privacy-sensitive ones, router in between. Multi-model is the default.

📚 Key Terms

Open weights
Model parameters are downloadable; you can run inference. Distinct from open source.
Open source
Weights + training data + pipeline + post-training recipe all public. No frontier model satisfies this in 2026.
Apache 2.0
The most permissive open-weight license — commercial use, redistribution, patent grant, no share-alike. Used by DeepSeek V4 and (since April 3, 2026) Gemma 4.
Custom community license
Hybrid licenses (Qwen, Kimi, GLM, Llama) that sound open but restrict competing services, redistribution, or commercial use above thresholds.
Mixture-of-Experts (MoE)
Architecture where only a fraction of parameters activate per token. Why open labs could ship 1.6T-parameter models on modest inference budgets.
Sovereign AI stack
End-to-end domestic AI infrastructure with no foreign software dependencies. DeepSeek V4 + Huawei Ascend + CANN is the first production example.
SWE-Bench Pro
Private-codebase variant of SWE-Bench. Where public scores hit 80%+, Pro’s private subset crashes top closed models to under 25%. The eval/production gap, quantified.
Distillation
Training a smaller/open model on synthetic data generated by a larger/closed model. Likely explains why open frontier scores arrive shortly after each closed-model release.

🚨 The Two Contrarian Threads

📂
Open weights ≠ auditable
Training data, post-training recipe, RLHF rewards, safety filters all stay closed. You get inference reproducibility, not training reproducibility — matters for regulators, security researchers, and enterprise legal.
🪞
Open is downstream of closed
Open frontier scores arrive suspiciously fast after closed-lab releases — strongly suggesting distillation. The capability lead in raw research still belongs to whoever has the strongest closed model that month.

🧪 Quick Quiz

Self-check

What’s the difference between “open weights” and “open source” in 2026?

Open weights = parameters downloadable; you can run and fine-tune. Open source = weights + training data + pipeline + post-training recipe all public. No frontier model is open source in 2026.

Which model leads Chatbot Arena in April 2026 — and what’s surprising about it?

GLM-5 from Zhipu AI at 1451 ELO — the highest of any model on the leaderboard, open or closed. Surprising because the human-preference leader in April 2026 is open-weight, Chinese, and trained on Huawei chips.

Where do closed labs still hold a real lead?

Production coding on private codebases (SWE-Bench Pro), long-horizon agentic work (Terminal-Bench, MCP-Atlas), and latency at scale. Open hasn’t closed these gaps yet.

What did the Huawei + DeepSeek announcement actually break?

The assumption that frontier LLM training requires Nvidia. DeepSeek V4 ships day-zero on Ascend 950 chips with CANN as a CUDA equivalent — an end-to-end Chinese stack with zero US software dependencies. Export controls have a workaround.

Why might most serious teams in 2026 choose multi-model over committing to one side?

Closed flagships still win on agentic and bleeding-edge tasks; open weights win on privacy, sovereignty, fine-tuning, cost-at-scale, and vendor independence. Different tasks, different right answers — routing wins.

🔑 Key Takeaways

  • Five Chinese labs (DeepSeek, Qwen, Kimi, GLM) plus Western open pack (Llama, Mistral, Gemma 4) simultaneously reached frontier quality in the last 18 months.
  • Open matches or leads closed on knowledge (MMLU), math (MATH-500), human preference (Chatbot Arena ELO — GLM-5 at #1).
  • Closed still leads on private-codebase coding (SWE-Bench Pro), long-horizon agentic, and latency at scale.
  • DeepSeek V4 + Huawei Ascend + CANN = first production-grade end-to-end Chinese AI stack with zero Nvidia dependencies.
  • License spectrum: Apache 2.0 (most permissive, DeepSeek/Gemma 4) → Llama Community → custom community (Qwen/Kimi/GLM) → closed-API. Read carefully.
  • “Open weights” ≠ “open source.” Training data, pipelines, and post-training recipes remain closed. Audit story is incomplete.
  • Likely strategic reality: open frontier is downstream of closed via distillation. Closed still leads raw research.
  • Production winners run multi-model: closed for the hardest tasks, open for privacy/scale, router in between.
