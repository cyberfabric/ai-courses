Embeddings Demystified

The Big Idea

Embeddings are GPS coordinates for meaning. They turn text into numbers where similar meanings become nearby points — letting you search by what things mean, not what words they contain.

🗺️ The GPS Coordinates Analogy

📍 Tokyo
(35.68, 139.69)
≈
📍 Osaka
(34.69, 135.50)
≠
📍 London
(51.51, -0.13)

GPS: 2 dimensions (lat, long) → physical proximity. Embeddings: 768–3072 dimensions → semantic proximity.

🧩 Core Concepts

🔢
Vector = List of Numbers
An embedding is 768–3072 floats representing meaning. Each dimension is like a slider for a different facet of meaning.
📐
Cosine Similarity
Measures the angle between two vectors. 1.0 = identical meaning, 0 = unrelated. Ignores vector length, focuses on direction.
🧠
Trained on Billions of Pairs
Models learn "related texts → close vectors" from billions of examples. They generalize to text they've never seen.
📏
Dimension Flexibility
OpenAI models let you request fewer dims (e.g., 256 vs 1536). Trades small quality drop for 6x less storage.

📐 Cosine Similarity Score Guide

Score Range Meaning RAG Relevance
0.85 – 1.0 Very similar ✅ Almost certainly relevant
0.70 – 0.85 Similar ✅ Likely relevant
0.50 – 0.70 Somewhat related ⚠️ Maybe relevant
Below 0.50 Probably not related ❌ Probably not relevant

Why cosine, not Euclidean? Cosine measures direction (meaning), ignoring length (text size).

⚠️ The Rules of Embedding Space

🚫
Never Mix Models
Vectors from different models live in different spaces. Index with model A → must query with model A. Switching = re-embed everything.
🏷️
Query Prefixes Matter
Some models need "query:" or "passage:" prefixes. Same text + different prefix = different vector. Check the docs.
📏
Dims ≠ Quality
3072 dims isn't automatically better than 1024. Cohere at 1024 outscores OpenAI at 3072 on MTEB benchmarks.
📦
Batch Your Calls
Send 100+ texts per API call, not one at a time. Up to 2048 texts per call for OpenAI models.

🐛 When Embeddings Fail

🔤 Acronyms & Jargon
Domain-specific terms ("DPLL", "WCET") may embed poorly. Fix: expand acronyms before embedding.
🆔 Exact Identifiers
Product IDs, error codes ("ERR-4021") are meaningless to embedding models. Fix: hybrid search (Ch. 7).
❗ Negation
"Product is great" vs "product is not great" may have similar embeddings. Models are improving but still weak here.
📝 Very Short Text
One-word chunks embed poorly — not enough context. Keep chunks at a meaningful length.

🧪 Quick Quiz

What is an embedding in one sentence?

A list of numbers (768–3072 floats) that represents the meaning of text, where similar meanings produce geometrically close vectors.

Why does cosine similarity beat Euclidean distance for RAG?

Cosine measures the angle (direction/meaning) between vectors, ignoring length. This matters because longer texts may produce longer vectors — you want to compare meaning, not text size.

Can you mix vectors from text-embedding-3-small and embed-v4?

No. Different models create completely different vector spaces. Comparing them is meaningless. Index and query must use the exact same model.

When would you choose an open-source embedding model over an API?

When you have data privacy requirements (data can't leave your network), need to control costs at scale, or want to fine-tune the model for your specific domain.

Why might embeddings fail on "ERR-4021" or "DPLL"?

Embedding models are trained on general text. Domain-specific acronyms and error codes aren't well-represented in training data. Fix with hybrid search (combine semantic + keyword) or expand acronyms before embedding.

🎒 Key Takeaways

  • Embeddings are GPS coordinates for meaning: similar text → nearby points in high-dimensional space.
  • Cosine similarity (0 to 1) measures "how close in meaning." Above 0.7 = likely relevant for RAG.
  • Start with text-embedding-3-small. Switch only for specific needs (multilingual, code, privacy).
  • Never mix models. Never forget query prefixes. Batch your API calls (up to 2048 per call).
  • Embeddings fail on acronyms, exact IDs, negation, and very short text — hybrid search helps.
  • Embedding quality determines retrieval quality. A bad embedding model breaks everything downstream.
