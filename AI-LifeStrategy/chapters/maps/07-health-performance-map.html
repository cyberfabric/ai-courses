🛏️ The Developer Sleep Protocol

  1. Target 7-9 hours of actual sleep

    Not time in bed — actual sleep. Add 15-30 min buffer for falling asleep.

  2. Fixed wake time — even weekends

    Your circadian rhythm anchors to your wake time. Consistency beats sleeping in.

  3. Screen curfew 60 min before bed

    Blue light suppresses melatonin. Read a book, stretch, journal instead.

  4. Cool, dark, quiet bedroom

    65-68°F (18-20°C). Blackout curtains. Earplugs or white noise.

  5. No caffeine after 2 PM

    Half-life of 5-6 hours. That 4 PM coffee is still in your system at 10 PM.

🔁 Building Habits That Stick

Habit Stacking
Attach new habits to existing ones: "After I pour coffee → 5 min stretching."
Two-Minute Rule
Make it so small it's impossible to fail. "Exercise 30 min" → "put on workout shoes."
Environment Design
Make good habits easy (fruit on counter) and bad habits hard (phone outside bedroom).
Never Miss Twice
One bad day = exception. Two bad days = new pattern. Miss Monday? Tuesday is non-negotiable.
Schedule Like a Meeting
"I'll exercise when I have time" = you won't. Put it on the calendar, treat it as mandatory.
Energy Audit
Track energy 1-10 every 2 hours for a week. Match task difficulty to energy level, not calendar.

🧪 Quick Quiz

Test Your Knowledge

What does 6 hours of sleep for 14 days do to your brain?

Produces the same cognitive impairment as being legally drunk. You think you're fine — you're not.

What's the non-negotiable minimum exercise for developers?

30 minutes of walking per day + 2 strength training sessions per week. This alone beats 80% of developers.

Why is caffeine after 2 PM a problem?

Caffeine's half-life is 5-6 hours. A 4 PM coffee is still in your system at 10 PM, disrupting sleep quality.

What's the "never miss twice" rule?

One bad day is an exception. Two bad days is a new pattern. If you skip Monday, Tuesday is non-negotiable.

How much does each notification cost you in refocus time?

23 minutes (UC Irvine research). Every ping is a context switch that destroys your deep work.

📌 Key Takeaways

If You Remember Nothing Else

  • Energy management beats time management — match task difficulty to energy level, not your calendar.
  • Sleep is the #1 intervention: 7-9 hours, fixed wake time, no screens before bed, no caffeine after 2 PM.
  • Exercise is a brain upgrade: 30-min daily walk + 2× strength training = ahead of 80% of developers.
  • Nutrition 80/20: whole foods, enough protein, veggies at 2 meals, stay hydrated, limit liquid calories.
  • Burnout creeps — catch it at overwork, not collapse. Boundaries and recovery blocks are not optional.
  • Your workspace is a medical device: invest $1,500-3,000 now or $10,000+ in medical bills later.
  • Never miss twice — the habit rule that separates lasting change from abandoned resolutions.
  • AI anxiety is real and normal. Limit news, focus on what you control, and therapy is maintenance, not crisis.
