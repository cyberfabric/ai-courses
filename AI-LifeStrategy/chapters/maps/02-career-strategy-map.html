📅 The 90-Day Career Upgrade

  1. Weeks 1-2: Define

    Choose your Pi-shape. Validate with 3 real job postings at the intersection.

  2. Weeks 2-3: Audit

    Categorize your top 10 skills as amplified, replaced, or immune. Shift investment.

  3. Weeks 3-4: Evaluate

    Score your company on green/red flags. Update resume if red outweighs green.

  4. Weeks 4-8: Build

    Solve one real problem with AI at work. Document it. Share results. Activate 10 network contacts.

  5. Weeks 8-12: Act

    Green flags? Negotiate raise/title/learning time using AI wins. Red flags? Start interviewing.

🧪 Quick Quiz

Test Your Understanding

Why is the T-shape no longer enough?

AI can reach "pretty good" in any single domain within seconds. Single-domain depth is no longer sufficient differentiation. The Pi-shape (two deep pillars + AI bridge) creates intersections that are exponentially harder to automate.

What are the three categories every skill falls into?

AI-Amplified (invest heavily — system design, code review, communication), AI-Replaced (stop investing — boilerplate, basic docs, simple testing), and AI-Immune (your moat — trust-building, politics, mentoring, judgment under uncertainty).

What's the trap in being the "AI person" at work?

Becoming the AI support desk instead of advancing your career. The fix: teach others to fish instead of fishing for them. Enablers get promoted; bottlenecks get burned out.

How should you negotiate compensation around AI productivity gains?

Don't say "I use AI tools." Quantify impact: "I reduced feature delivery time by 40%." Charge for outcomes, not hours. Don't let employers pocket all the productivity gains.

What's a Career Moat Statement?

A written declaration of your unique intersection: "[primary expertise] + [second pillar] + [AI leverage] + [unique value] + [trusted network]." If you can't fill it in, that's your signal for where the gaps are.

📌 If You Remember Nothing Else

Key Takeaways

  • The Pi-shape beats the T-shape: two deep pillars + AI fluency. Intersections are exponentially harder to automate.
  • Categorize every skill as AI-amplified, replaced, or immune — then shift your investment accordingly.
  • If your entire value is "I write clean code fast," you're in trouble. Code is your tool, not your identity.
  • Be the AI person who enables others, not the bottleneck who does all the AI work.
  • Read the signals: green flags = invest and grow. Red flags = build exit options before you need them.
  • One employer = single point of failure. Build a portfolio career alongside your main job.
  • Negotiate for outcomes, not hours. Quantify your AI-powered impact with real numbers.
  • Write your Career Moat Statement. If you can't fill it in, you know exactly where to focus.
