💻 The Developer Edge — And Its Limits

✅ Developer Advantages ⚠️ Developer Blind Spots
You understand how AI tools actually work Overvaluing technical depth, undervaluing business acumen
You can build on top of AI APIs Building cool tools nobody wants to pay for
You think in systems and workflows Ignoring soft skills — "the code speaks for itself"
You can evaluate AI output critically Assuming technical skills alone = job security
You can automate your own AI workflows Staying in the code while others move to strategy

Bottom line: A technical background is a head start, not a finish line.

📖 Key Terms

Three Waves
AI's progression: automating tasks → thinking → decisions. Each wave reshapes different job categories.
10x Human
A person who uses AI as a force multiplier to produce 10x output — applies to everyone, not just coders.
Leverage Economy
The post-attention economy where value = output per input, amplified by AI and distribution.
Second-Order Effects
Not "what does this tech do?" but "what does it make possible?" — where the real winners position themselves.
Meta-Skills
Skills that AI amplifies rather than replaces: problem definition, taste, communication, systems thinking, relationships.
Compounding with AI
The winning mindset: AI skills build on each other over time, creating exponentially growing advantages.
Active Strategist
Someone who deliberately chooses where and how AI amplifies their life, vs. passively consuming AI-curated services.

🧪 Quick Quiz

Test Your Understanding

What are the three waves of AI?

Wave 1: Automation of tasks. Wave 2: Automation of thinking. Wave 3: Automation of decisions. The strategy is to always be one wave ahead.

What lesson does the electricity/internet/mobile pattern teach?

Technical understanding is necessary but not sufficient. The real winners understand second-order effects — not what the technology does, but what it makes possible.

What's the difference between the old economy equation and the new one?

Old: Output = Time × Skill. New: Output = Time × Skill × AI Leverage × Distribution. You need to maximize all four.

Why is "doing the same tasks faster with AI" the wrong strategy?

Because everyone gets that speedup. The real 10x advantage goes to people who use AI to do things they couldn't do before — new capabilities, not just faster old ones.

What's the most dangerous position in the AI job market?

Not "my job will be replaced" — it's "my job will be transformed by AI and I'll still be doing it the old way." You get replaced by colleagues who learned AI, not by AI itself.

📌 If You Remember Nothing Else

Key Takeaways

  • AI arrives in three waves (tasks → thinking → decisions). Always stay one wave ahead of your current role.
  • Winners of tech shifts understand second-order effects — not the tool, but what it makes possible.
  • The 10x advantage goes to people who use AI for new capabilities, not just doing old tasks faster.
  • The leverage equation: Output = Time × Skill × AI Leverage × Distribution. Maximize all four.
  • Five meta-skills AI amplifies: problem definition, taste, communication, systems thinking, relationships.
  • AI skills compound. The gap between users and non-users widens every month you wait.
  • Be an active strategist, not a passive consumer. AI is something you use for you — if you're intentional.
