💡 The One Thing

🔄

Build a Learning System, Not a Learning Event

The developers who thrive won't be the ones who learned the right thing today — they'll be the ones who can learn anything faster than everyone else when the time comes. Build loops, not one-offs.

⏳ The Knowledge Half-Life Crisis

📉
2.5-Year Half-Life
Half of what you know becomes outdated in ~2.5 years (was 5 years in 2010). AI is accelerating this.
🔮
85% Jobs Don't Exist Yet
Most jobs in 2030 haven't been invented. You can't learn "the right thing" — you need to learn how to learn.
⚡
6-Month AI Cycles
Major AI tool updates and capability jumps happen roughly every 6 months. Standing still = falling behind.

🧠 The Four Principles of Fast Learning

🔁
Active Recall
Close the material, explain from memory. 50-100% more effective than re-reading. Write down 3 key things after every session.
📅
Spaced Repetition
Revisit at intervals: 1 day → 3 days → 7 → 14. Use Anki. 10 min/day beats 2 hours of cramming weekly.
🏗️
Project-Based
Define a mini-project BEFORE you start learning. "I'll learn Rust by building a CLI tool." Purpose beats curriculum.
🎓
Teaching (Feynman)
Can't explain it simply? You don't understand it. Blog posts, videos, explaining to colleagues — teaching doubles as learning AND branding.

⚖️ Just-in-Time vs. Just-in-Case

Aspect Just-in-Case (20%) Just-in-Time (80%)
What Things you might need someday Things you need right now
When Weekly learning slot — exploratory Current project needs — targeted
Examples Pi-shape pillar, system design, leadership OAuth for this sprint, new framework
Danger Tutorial hell — learning without applying Shallow knowledge — hacking, not mastering

⏰ The 5-Hour Rule: Weekly Schedule

  1. 🔭 Monday — Explore (45 min)

    Read about your Pi-shape second pillar. Slow, exploratory just-in-case learning.

  2. 🏗️ Tuesday — Build (45 min)

    Work on your learning project. Hands-on, project-based practice.

  3. 🔗 Wednesday — Reflect (30 min)

    Process notes, connect ideas, update your knowledge management system.

  4. 🎓 Thursday — Teach (45 min)

    Write a blog post or explain something to a colleague. Teaching = deep learning.

  5. 🔁 Friday — Review (30 min)

    Spaced repetition and revisit the week's learnings. Consolidate.

  6. 🎯 Weekend — Deep Work (90 min)

    One focused session on a larger learning project. = ~260 hours/year total.

📖 Key Terms

Knowledge Half-Life
Time for half your knowledge to become outdated — now ~2.5 years for tech skills.
Meta-Learning
Learning how to learn — the skill that makes all other learning faster.
Active Recall
Testing yourself from memory instead of re-reading. 50-100% more effective.
Spaced Repetition
Reviewing at expanding intervals (1, 3, 7, 14 days) to lock in long-term memory.
Feynman Technique
Explain a concept in simple terms — if you can't, you don't really understand it.
Pi-Shape
Deep expertise in two areas plus broad AI fluency (from Chapter 2). Guides what to learn.
5-Hour Rule
5 hours/week of deliberate, structured learning. = 260 hours/year of compound growth.
Second Brain
A personal knowledge management system: Capture → Process → Connect → Retrieve → Create.

🧪 Quick Quiz

Test Yourself

What's the optimal balance between just-in-time and just-in-case learning?

80% just-in-time (learn what you need for current projects) and 20% just-in-case (exploratory learning in your weekly slot).

Why is "active recall" better than re-reading your notes?

Active recall — closing the material and explaining from memory — is 50-100% more effective because it forces your brain to reconstruct the information, strengthening neural pathways.

What's the key rule for using AI as a learning tool?

"If AI did the thinking, you didn't learn." Use AI as a Socratic tutor that asks questions and guides discovery — not as an answer machine you copy-paste from.

What are the 5 steps of the "Second Brain" knowledge loop?

Capture → Process (summarize in your own words) → Connect (link to existing knowledge) → Retrieve (search notes before Googling) → Create (turn notes into outputs).

Which Tier 1 skills should you learn NOW for the highest ROI?

AI Tool Fluency (2-4 weeks), System Design & Architecture (3-6 months), and Technical Communication (ongoing). These make every other skill more valuable.

📌 Key Takeaways

If You Remember Nothing Else

  • Build a learning system (loops), not learning events (one-off courses).
  • Four principles: Active Recall → Spaced Repetition → Project-Based → Teaching.
  • 80% just-in-time learning (current needs) + 20% just-in-case (exploratory).
  • Use AI as a Socratic tutor — if AI did the thinking, you didn't learn.
  • 5 hours/week of deliberate learning = 260 hours/year of compound growth.
  • Soft skills (communication, negotiation, leadership, empathy) have the longest shelf life.
  • One completed project teaches more than five abandoned courses.
  • Start the 12-Week Learning Sprint: pick ONE goal, define a mini-project, ship it.
