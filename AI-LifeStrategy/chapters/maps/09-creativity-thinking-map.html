📖 Key Terms

First Principles Thinking
Breaking a problem down to fundamental truths and reasoning up, instead of reasoning by analogy.
Inversion
Instead of "How do I succeed?" ask "How would I guarantee failure?" Then avoid those things.
Second-Order Thinking
Asking "And then what?" — thinking 2-3 steps ahead instead of just one.
The Map Is Not the Territory
All models are simplifications. AI output looks like knowledge but is a statistical model — don't confuse map for reality.
The Taste Gap
Your ability to tell good from great among AI-generated options. Developed through creating, studying excellence, and honest feedback.
Cross-Pollination
Importing ideas from unrelated domains — hobbies, reading, diverse experiences — into your primary work.
Systems Thinking
Seeing how components interact: feedback loops, emergence, unintended consequences. The big picture AI misses.
10/10/10 Rule
How will I feel about this decision in 10 minutes? 10 months? 10 years? Separates real decisions from anxiety.

🛠️ The Creative Stack for Developers

  1. Morning Pages / Journaling

    10-15 min freewriting each morning. Separates the "generating" brain from the "judging" brain.

  2. Wide Reading

    Read outside your field — history, psychology, biology, fiction. Diverse input = original output.

  3. Building for Fun

    Side projects with no business case. When there's no pressure to monetize, creativity is unleashed.

  4. Walking Without Input

    No podcast, no phone. Stanford research: walking increases creative output by 60%.

🎯 Your Creativity Action Plan

  1. Start Daily Writing — This Week

    15 minutes each morning. Journal, work log, or freewriting. Writing is thinking.

  2. Learn 3 Mental Models — Weeks 1-2

    First principles, inversion, second-order thinking. Apply each to a real decision.

  3. Start a Creative Hobby — Month 1

    Something physical, hands-on, non-screen. One session per week minimum.

  4. Read Outside Your Field — Ongoing

    One book per month: psychology, history, philosophy, or great fiction.

  5. Publish Something — Month 2

    Blog post, newsletter, Twitter thread. Forces clear thinking for an audience.

✅ Quick Quiz

What's the difference between AI "combination" and human "origination" on the creativity spectrum?

Combination means recombining existing approaches from different domains (AI can do this if prompted). Origination means deciding what problem is worth solving or what product the world is missing — it requires feeling the market and having skin in the game, which AI lacks.

Why is "writing as thinking" a superpower in the AI era?

AI writes faster and more, but writing clearly means thinking clearly. When you force ideas into words, you discover gaps in logic and assumptions you haven't examined. Clear, original thinking expressed precisely is what separates leaders from followers.

What is the "taste gap" and why does it matter?

The taste gap (from Ira Glass) is the difference between your ability to recognize quality and your ability to produce it. In the AI era, taste becomes your most valuable asset: AI can generate 100 options, but knowing which one is actually good — that's taste. It develops through practice, exposure, and honest self-assessment.

What's the difference between a Type 1 and Type 2 decision?

Type 1 = one-way door (irreversible or very costly to reverse) — analyze deeply, take your time. Type 2 = two-way door (easily reversible) — decide fast, iterate. Most people agonize over Type 2 decisions and rush Type 1s. Flip it.

How should you use AI as a thinking partner without atrophying your own skills?

Use AI to challenge and expand your thinking — as devil's advocate, for assumption testing, perspective shifting, and idea expansion. The key: use it like a sparring partner that pushes you, but you do the fighting. Don't outsource your thinking to AI or the very skill that makes you valuable will atrophy.

📋 Key Takeaways

  • Creativity isn't painting — it's seeing connections others miss, framing problems differently, and generating novel solutions.
  • Move right on the creativity spectrum: from Execution → Vision. The further right, the more AI-proof your value.
  • Master mental models: first principles, inversion, second-order thinking, map vs territory.
  • Writing clearly IS thinking clearly — start a daily writing practice.
  • Develop taste through volume, studying excellence, and honest feedback. Taste is the new technical skill.
  • Cross-pollinate: one serious hobby outside tech feeds creativity you can't get from more screen time.
  • Use AI as a sparring partner for your thinking — never as a replacement for it.
  • Be contrarian when consensus is driven by emotion, not evidence.
