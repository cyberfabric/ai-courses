Your 90-Day AI Life Strategy

The playbook — pull everything together into a concrete, actionable plan

From Strategy to Execution

You've made it. Eleven chapters of strategy covering career, income, branding, learning, money, health, relationships, creativity, resilience, and future-proofing. That's an enormous amount of ground.

Now here's the hard truth: none of it matters if you don't execute.

This chapter is different from the others. It's not about new ideas. It's about taking everything you've learned and compressing it into a concrete 90-day plan that you start today. Not next Monday. Not after the next sprint. Today.

Ninety days is the perfect planning horizon. Long enough to make real progress. Short enough to stay focused. And it creates a rhythm: plan, execute, review, repeat. Four cycles per year, each building on the last.

The Life Strategy Canvas

Before diving into the weekly plan, zoom out. Your Life Strategy Canvas is a one-page snapshot of where you are and where you're heading across six life dimensions.

Life Strategy Canvas — The Six Dimensions

Career: Your professional trajectory, skills, and positioning. (Chapters 1-2)

Money: Income streams, savings, investments, financial runway. (Chapters 3, 6)

Health: Sleep, exercise, nutrition, mental health, energy. (Chapter 7)

Relationships: Close ties, professional network, community, family. (Chapter 8)

Growth: Learning, creativity, thinking skills, personal brand. (Chapters 4, 5, 9)

Purpose: Values, resilience, meaning, future-proofing. (Chapters 10, 11)

For each dimension, you need three things: where you are now (honest assessment), where you want to be in 90 days (specific target), and the one action that moves the needle most (your lever).

Month 1: Foundation

The first month is about getting the basics right. No moonshots yet — just solid foundations that everything else builds on.

1
Health habits. Fix your sleep (fixed wake time, screen curfew). Start walking 30 minutes daily. Two strength sessions per week. Fill a water bottle at your desk. This is the foundation for everything — nothing else works if your energy is broken.
Week 1-2
2
Financial audit. Calculate your runway number (months of expenses in savings). Set up automatic investment contributions if you haven't. Kill any high-interest debt. Review your spending for the past 3 months — where's money leaking?
Week 1-2
3
Career moat statement. Write one sentence: "I am hard to replace because ___." If you can't finish that sentence, that's your first career priority. Identify the skills or positioning that make you uniquely valuable.
Week 2
4
Learning sprint start. Pick ONE skill to develop over the next 12 weeks. Not three. One. Allocate 5 hours per week. Schedule it like a meeting. Start the first session.
Week 2-3
5
Relationship map. List your 5 closest relationships, 5 professional allies, and identify gaps. Reach out to 3 dormant connections. Schedule one recurring social commitment.
Week 3-4
Month 1 will feel slow. You're not "achieving" visible results — you're laying pipe. Financial audits, health habits, and relationship mapping are not exciting. But they're the infrastructure that makes months 2 and 3 possible. Trust the process. The builders who pour a solid foundation before raising walls don't get applause during the pour, but their building stands.

Month 2: Build

Foundation is solid. Now start building on it.

1
Launch a side income experiment. Not a business — an experiment. Freelance one project. Create one digital product. Offer one consulting session. The goal isn't revenue (yet) — it's learning whether this income stream has potential.
Week 5-6
2
Publish your first content. A blog post, a LinkedIn article, a Twitter thread, a newsletter issue. Topic: something you learned in month 1 or from your learning sprint. It doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to exist.
Week 5-6
3
Deepen 3 key relationships. One close friend, one professional ally, one potential mentor. Have a real conversation — not a LinkedIn message. Coffee, video call, or in-person. Go deeper than surface level.
Week 5-8
4
Upgrade your workspace. Get the ergonomic chair, the proper monitor setup, the keyboard that won't wreck your wrists. Your body is the machine everything else runs on. Invest in the hardware.
Week 6-7
5
Start a creative practice. 15 minutes daily: journaling, sketching, building something for fun, learning an instrument. Creative cross-pollination feeds everything else.
Week 7-8

Month 3: Accelerate

Now the system is running. Month 3 is about evaluating what's working, doubling down on winners, and setting up the next cycle.

1
Evaluate your experiments. Did your side income experiment show promise? Double down. Was it a dead end? Kill it fast and try a different approach next quarter. No shame in pivoting — that's what experiments are for.
Week 9-10
2
Double down on winners. Whatever's working — the content that got traction, the skill that's clicking, the relationship that's energizing — invest more time there. Prune what isn't working.
Week 10-11
3
Complete your learning sprint. Ship something visible from your 12-week skill investment. A project, a presentation, an article demonstrating your new capability. Learning without output is invisible.
Week 11-12
4
Retake the Future-Proof Score. Compare to your baseline from Chapter 11. Where did you improve? Where are you still vulnerable? Your weakest dimension becomes next quarter's priority.
Week 12
5
Plan the next 90 days. Update your Life Strategy Canvas. Set new targets for each dimension. Choose your next learning sprint. Schedule the next quarter's experiments.
Week 12

The Weekly Review Template

A 90-day plan without weekly check-ins is a wish list. The weekly review is your steering wheel. 15 minutes every Sunday.

The 15-Minute Weekly Review
  1. Score the week (2 min): Rate each dimension 1-5. Career ___ Money ___ Health ___ Relationships ___ Growth ___ Purpose ___
  2. Three wins (2 min): What went well? Write three things, no matter how small. This trains your brain to notice progress.
  3. One lesson (2 min): What did you learn or what would you do differently?
  4. Habit check (3 min): Did you hit your non-negotiables? Sleep, exercise, learning, social connection? If you missed twice in a row on anything, that's a red flag.
  5. Next week's #1 priority (3 min): What's the single most important thing to accomplish? Not the most urgent — the most important.
  6. Energy check (3 min): How's your energy? If it's low, what needs to change? More sleep? Less caffeine? A day off? Prevention is cheaper than burnout recovery.
Do the weekly review at the same time every week. Sunday evening works well — it sets you up for Monday with clarity. Keep a dedicated notebook or document. Over months, you'll build a powerful record of your trajectory. Patterns emerge that you'd never notice week to week: seasonal energy dips, recurring blockers, what actually moves the needle vs. what feels productive.

The AI Life Stack

Here are the recommended tools for each life dimension. All are AI-powered or AI-enhanced:

💼 Career

Claude/ChatGPT for career strategy, interview prep, and skill gap analysis. LinkedIn for visibility. GitHub for portfolio. Glassdoor for salary research.

💰 Money

YNAB or Monarch for budgeting. Vanguard/Fidelity for automated investing. Claude for financial modeling and scenario analysis. TurboTax or an AI tax tool for optimization.

🏃 Health

Oura Ring or Apple Watch for sleep and activity tracking. MacroFactor for nutrition. Headspace or Waking Up for meditation. Strong or Hevy for workout tracking.

🤝 Relationships

A personal CRM (Clay, Dex, or a simple spreadsheet). Calendar blocks for social time. Claude for conversation prep and thoughtful message drafting.

🧠 Growth

Obsidian or Notion for knowledge management. Claude as a learning tutor. Readwise for highlights. Substack or Ghost for publishing. Anki for spaced repetition.

🧭 Purpose

Day One or a paper journal for reflection. Rosebud for AI-assisted journaling. A values card pinned above your desk. Your weekly review template.

Use AI as your 90-day strategy co-pilot.
  • Weekly review assistant: "Here are my scores and notes from this week. What patterns do you notice? Where should I adjust?"
  • Experiment evaluation: "I tried X for 4 weeks and here are my results [data]. Is this worth continuing or should I pivot? What would a stronger version of this experiment look like?"
  • Quarterly planning: "Here's my Life Strategy Canvas. My weakest areas are [X] and [Y]. Design a focused 90-day improvement plan for those two areas."
  • Accountability partner: "I committed to publishing one blog post per week. I've written 2 in the last month. Help me diagnose why I'm falling short and design a more realistic commitment."
  • Annual review: "Here are my four quarterly reviews from this year. Summarize my trajectory, identify the biggest wins and persistent weaknesses, and suggest strategic priorities for next year."
AI doesn't replace self-awareness or discipline. But it makes reflection sharper, planning more thorough, and course-correction faster.

Common Failure Modes (and How to Recover)

Knowing what goes wrong is as important as knowing what to do. Here are the traps that derail most 90-day plans:

Failure Mode 1: Overcommitting

The symptom: You try to optimize all six dimensions simultaneously in week one. You burn out by week three.

The fix: Pick TWO dimensions to focus on per quarter. The others get maintenance effort only. You can't renovate every room in the house at once.

Failure Mode 2: Shiny Object Syndrome

The symptom: Every week there's a new AI tool, a new side hustle idea, a new skill to learn. You start everything and finish nothing.

The fix: Commit to your chosen experiment and learning sprint for the full 90 days. Write it down. When a shiny new idea appears, add it to a "next quarter" list. Revisit the list during your quarterly review — most items will have lost their shine.

Failure Mode 3: Perfectionism Paralysis

The symptom: You don't publish the blog post because it's not good enough. You don't launch the side project because it needs one more feature. You don't reach out to the contact because you haven't perfected your pitch.

The fix: Ship at 80%. The first version of anything should embarrass you slightly. If it doesn't, you waited too long. Done beats perfect. Every. Single. Time.

Failure Mode 4: Isolation

The symptom: You're executing the plan alone. No one knows what you're working on. No one asks how it's going. Accountability evaporates.

The fix: Tell someone. Share your 90-day targets with one person. Do a monthly check-in. Accountability dramatically increases follow-through.

You will not execute this plan perfectly. That is fine. The goal is not 100% completion. The goal is consistent forward motion. If you do 60% of what's in this chapter, you'll be ahead of 95% of people who worry about AI but do nothing about it. Progress over perfection. Momentum over milestones. The difference between people who change their lives and people who don't isn't talent or resources — it's showing up imperfectly, week after week, for long enough that the compound effect kicks in.

Accountability Systems

Willpower is finite. Systems are not. Build accountability into the structure, not the effort.

Accountability Options (Pick at Least One)

  • Accountability partner. One person who you check in with weekly or biweekly. Share your targets, share your progress, be honest about what you missed. Mutual accountability works best — they share their goals too.
  • Public commitment. Post your 90-day goals somewhere public — Twitter, a blog, a team Slack channel. Social pressure is a powerful motivator. Update monthly on progress.
  • Skin in the game. Put money on it. StickK or Beeminder let you commit money to a charity you dislike if you fail to meet your targets. Sounds extreme. Works remarkably well.
  • Community. Join or create a group of people pursuing similar goals. Mastermind groups, Slack communities, cohort-based courses. Seeing others make progress is contagious.
Accountability without flexibility becomes a prison. Life happens — a sick kid, a work crisis, a bad week. Build in "grace days." The rule from Chapter 7 applies: never miss twice. One bad week is normal. Two bad weeks in a row is a pattern that needs attention. Three is a system that needs redesign.

The Annual Life Strategy Review

Once a year — December or your birthday work well — zoom all the way out. This is not a weekly review. It's a strategic assessment of your entire life trajectory.

Annual Review Questions

  1. What am I most proud of this year?
  2. What was my biggest lesson or failure?
  3. How did each life dimension change? (Career, money, health, relationships, growth, purpose)
  4. What should I stop doing? (Activities, commitments, or habits that no longer serve me)
  5. What should I start doing? (New experiments, skills, or commitments for next year)
  6. What should I continue doing? (Systems and habits that are working)
  7. Is my career moat still strong? Do I need to adjust my positioning?
  8. How has the AI landscape shifted, and does my strategy need updating?
  9. Who are the 5 people who had the most positive impact on my year?
  10. If next year were my last year, what would I prioritize differently?

Beyond 90 Days

Your first 90-day cycle is the hardest. You're building systems from scratch, fighting inertia, and figuring out what works for you specifically. After the first cycle, it gets easier — because systems are now running, habits are forming, and you have data on what actually moves the needle.

The Quarterly Rhythm

Q2: Build
→
Q3: Accelerate
→
Q4: Review + Plan

Each quarter, two things happen: you maintain what's working (systems on autopilot) and you focus on 1-2 new priorities (based on your lowest Future-Proof Score dimensions). Over four quarters, you've meaningfully improved every dimension of your life. Over several years, you've compounded into a completely different position.

Your strategy should evolve as fast as AI does. The 90-day cycle is not a one-time event — it's an operating system for your life. Each quarter, reassess the AI landscape. What changed? What new tools exist? What roles are emerging? What skills are depreciating? Update your strategy accordingly. The people who set a plan in 2024 and follow it rigidly through 2027 will be misaligned. The people who plan in 90-day cycles, course-correcting each quarter, will stay aligned with a rapidly shifting landscape. Flexibility is not indecision. It's intelligence applied to an uncertain world.
