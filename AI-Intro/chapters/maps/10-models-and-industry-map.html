🔓 Open vs. Closed Source

Aspect Closed Source (GPT-4, Claude) Open Weights (Llama, Mistral)
Access API only Download & run yourself
Quality Cutting-edge (usually) Strong but generally behind frontier
Privacy Data sent to provider Full control, nothing leaves your infra
Customization Limited (prompt engineering) Fine-tune for your use case
Cost model Pay per token Hardware costs (no per-token fees)
Infrastructure Managed by provider You manage GPUs & scaling

⚠️ "Open weights" ≠ "open source" ≠ "open training data." Most "open" models only share weights, not training code or data.

🎨 Analogies Worth Remembering

🚗 The Car Lineup (Model Sizes)
7B = economy car (handles daily commutes). 70B = SUV (complex terrain). 200B+ = supercar (peak performance, costs a fortune). Pick based on your actual needs, not bragging rights.
🔧 Swiss Army Knife vs. Dedicated Tool (Specialization)
General models (GPT-4, Claude) do everything reasonably well. Specialized models (CodeLlama, DeepSeek Coder) do one thing extremely well — and cost a fraction. A 7B code specialist can beat a 70B generalist on programming.
🎨 Sculptor (Diffusion)
Image generation starts with noise (random marble) and chips away guided by your prompt until a coherent image (statue) appears. Fundamentally different from LLMs — not next-token prediction.
🔗 Three Models in a Trenchcoat (Voice Assistants)
Voice mode = Whisper (speech→text) + LLM (text→response) + TTS (response→speech). Three separate AI models chained together, each doing one thing well.

💡 The Big Idea

💡

Pick the Right Tool, Not the Biggest One

The AI landscape has models for every task and every budget. A 7B specialist can outperform a 200B generalist on focused tasks — and cost 100× less. Cloud APIs for convenience and frontier quality, local models for privacy and cost control, and often both together. Understanding the landscape means you pick based on actual needs: quality, privacy, cost, and complexity — not hype.

🧪 Quick Quiz

Test Yourself

How do image generation models differ from LLMs?

LLMs use next-token prediction (generate text one token at a time). Image models use diffusion — they start with random noise and gradually refine it into a coherent image, guided by the text prompt. Both are neural networks trained on massive data, but the architecture and process are fundamentally different.

What's the difference between "open weights" and "open source"?

Open weights means you can download and run the model. Open source would mean the training data, training code, and data curation process are also shared — allowing full reproduction. Most "open" models only share weights. True open-source AI (everything reproducible) is rare.

When would you choose a local model over a cloud API?

When privacy is critical (sensitive data can't leave your infrastructure), when you need offline access, when per-token costs add up at high volume, or when you want to fine-tune for a specific use case. Trade-off: you manage the infrastructure and hardware.

Can a 7B model ever be better than a 200B model?

Yes — on specialized tasks. A 7B model fine-tuned specifically for code can outperform a 200B generalist on programming tasks, while costing a fraction to run. General models are Swiss Army knives; specialized models are dedicated tools. Pick based on your actual task.

How does a voice assistant like ChatGPT voice mode work?

It chains three separate models: Whisper (speech-to-text) converts your voice to text, an LLM generates a text response, and a TTS model converts the response back to speech. Three AI models working in sequence, each doing one thing well.

📌 Key Takeaways

If You Remember Nothing Else

  • The AI world is bigger than LLMs: image (diffusion), speech (Whisper/TTS), code (CodeLlama), multimodal (GPT-4o, Gemini), and embeddings.
  • Closed source (OpenAI, Anthropic) = cutting-edge quality via API. Open weights (Llama, Mistral) = control, privacy, fine-tuning.
  • "Open weights" ≠ "open source." Most "open" models only share weights, not training data or code.
  • Model sizes: 1B (phone) → 7B (laptop GPU) → 70B (server) → 200B+ (cloud). Bigger ≠ always better for your task.
  • A practical setup: cloud APIs for complex/frontier tasks + local 7B model (Ollama) for simple/private tasks.
  • The landscape changes every few months. Prices drop, capabilities rise. What matters is understanding the trade-offs.
