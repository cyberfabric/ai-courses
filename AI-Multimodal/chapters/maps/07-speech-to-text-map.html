The Big Idea

Audio is secretly an image problem. Sound waves become spectrograms (2D images of frequency over time), and then the same encoder-decoder architecture used in vision and language generates text. One model — Whisper — handles 100 languages, runs locally for free, and changed everything.

🧩 Core Concepts

📊
Spectrogram
A 2D heat map of audio: x-axis = time, y-axis = frequency, brightness = loudness. Turns sound into an "image" that models can process.
🎤
Whisper
OpenAI's open-source STT model. Trained on 680K hours of audio. 100 languages, auto-punctuation, translation built in. The game-changer.
👥
Speaker Diarization
Identifies who said what. Clusters voice characteristics into Speaker 1, Speaker 2, etc. Separate from transcription — usually a second model (pyannote).
🏷️
Audio Classification
Not all audio is speech. Classifying sounds: music vs speech, emotions, acoustic scenes, environmental sounds. Same encoder, different output.
🌍
Multilingual + Translation
Whisper auto-detects language and can translate any of 100 languages to English in a single pass — no separate translation step.
⚡
faster-whisper
The go-to for self-hosted Whisper. Uses CTranslate2, runs 4x faster with same accuracy. Same API — just swap the import.
🔗
Audio + LLM Combo
Transcribe with Whisper, then feed text to an LLM for summarization, Q&A, action extraction, sentiment. The most practical multimodal pipeline.
🎧
Native Audio Models
Gemini 1.5 Pro (up to 9.5 hrs audio) and GPT-4o accept audio directly — no transcription step needed. Understand speech, music, and sounds.

Use a fast streaming model (Deepgram) for real-time display, then re-process the complete audio with Whisper for a polished final transcript. Best of both worlds.

⚠️ Common Challenges

🔊 Background Noise
Preprocess with noise reduction. Directional mics help more than software fixes for real-time.
🗣️ Accents & Dialects
Common accents work well. Thick regional dialects? Fine-tune Whisper on a few hours of domain audio.
🏢 Technical Jargon
"Kubernetes" → "Cooper Netties." Fix with post-processing: custom dictionary or LLM correction with expected terms.
👥 Overlapping Speech
People talking over each other kills accuracy. Hardware fix: separate mic channels per person.
🌐 Low-Resource Languages
Whisper quality varies — great for English/Spanish/French, weaker for rare languages. Always test first.

🧪 Quick Quiz

Test Your Understanding

What is a spectrogram and why is it important for audio AI?

A spectrogram is a 2D image showing which frequencies are present at each moment in time (x = time, y = frequency, brightness = loudness). It's important because it converts audio into an "image," allowing vision-like techniques (encoders, transformers) to process sound.

What makes Whisper different from older STT systems?

Whisper was trained on 680K hours of diverse internet audio (77 years!). It handles 100 languages, accents, noise, and code-switching. It's open source and produces properly punctuated, cased text — unlike older systems that output ALL CAPS WITH NO PUNCTUATION.

What's the difference between transcription and diarization?

Transcription tells you what was said (words). Diarization tells you who said it (speaker labels). They're separate tasks — Whisper does transcription, pyannote.audio does diarization. You align their outputs by timestamps.

When should you use batch vs real-time transcription?

Batch for recorded files (meetings, podcasts, voicemail) — higher accuracy, uses Whisper. Real-time for live scenarios (captions, voice assistants, dictation) — lower latency, uses Deepgram/AssemblyAI/Google. The hybrid approach uses both.

How do you handle Whisper mangling your company's jargon?

Post-processing: use a custom dictionary for find-and-replace of known misspellings, or send the transcript to an LLM with a list of expected terms for intelligent correction.

Key Takeaways

  • Audio → spectrogram (2D image) → encoder → decoder → text. Audio AI borrows heavily from vision AI.
  • Whisper: 100 languages, 680K hours training, open source, auto-punctuation. The small model is the sweet spot for most uses.
  • Diarization (who spoke) is separate from transcription (what was said) — combine Whisper + pyannote for full speaker-tagged transcripts.
  • Batch = higher accuracy (Whisper). Real-time = lower latency (Deepgram, AssemblyAI). Hybrid = best of both.
  • The killer combo: Whisper + LLM. Transcribe, then summarize, extract actions, answer questions — the most practical multimodal pipeline you can build today.
  • Native audio models (Gemini 1.5 Pro, GPT-4o) skip transcription entirely — send audio directly, ask questions about it.
