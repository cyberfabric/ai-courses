Energy & Thermal Optimization

Memory Map
💡

The Big Idea

Energy bugs are invisible during development — your phone is plugged in while you test. Users in the real world see a completely different energy profile. Profile wirelessly, use MetricKit for production truth, and think of energy like a thermostat: burst work then idle beats constant low-level polling.

🔋 The 5 Silent Battery Drains

  1. 📍 Aggressive Location Tracking

    kCLLocationAccuracyBest = GPS always on. Use significantLocationChanges unless you’re building navigation.

  2. ⏰ Timer/Polling That Never Stops

    1-second polling = CPU never sleeps. Replace with push notifications or WebSockets. Invalidate timers on view disappear.

  3. 🔄 Background Refresh Abuse

    BGAppRefreshTask downloading 20 MB the user may never see. Keep under 30s and <1 MB.

  4. 🎨 Unnecessary GPU Work

    Blur effects, real-time shadows, continuous off-screen animations. Pause when not visible.

  5. 📡 Uncompressed Payloads

    Raw JSON over cellular burns radio energy. Enable gzip. Serve WebP/HEIC instead of PNG.

🌡️ Thermal State Response Guide

State What It Means Your App Should
.nominal Everything fine Full features, full quality
.fair Slightly elevated temp Reduce background work, lower animation frame rate
.serious CPU/GPU throttled Disable non-essential features, stop prefetching, reduce image quality
.critical May force-suspend apps Minimal operation only. Save state. Prepare for suspension.

📖 Key Terms

Power Profiler
Xcode 26 instrument showing subsystem-level power attribution (CPU, GPU, Display, Network, Location) correlated with code.
Thermal state
Four levels: nominal, fair, serious, critical. Monitor via ProcessInfo.thermalState. Degrade gracefully as temperature rises.
CPU wake
Each time the CPU activates from idle. High wakes/minute = timer or polling preventing sleep. Target: idle periods between interactions.
MetricKit MXCPUMetric
Production metric for cumulative CPU time and instructions. Compare across versions to detect energy regressions.
BGAppRefreshTask
Lightweight background fetch scheduled by the system at optimal times. Keep under 30s and <1 MB.
BGProcessingTask
Heavy background work (ML, database cleanup). Set requiresExternalPower and requiresNetworkConnectivity to run while charging on WiFi.
significantLocationChanges
Cell-tower-based location updates. Low energy, ~500m accuracy. Use instead of continuous GPS for most apps.
Wireless debugging
Required for accurate Power Profiler readings. USB charging zeroes out power metrics.

🧪 Quick Quiz

Why must you use wireless debugging when profiling energy?

When the device is connected via USB, it’s charging. The Power Profiler reports zero power consumption because the device is receiving power, not draining it. Wireless debugging gives you real battery impact measurements.

What’s the “thermostat analogy” for energy efficiency?

Burst work then idle (like quick heating then off) is more efficient than constant low-level work (heater on 24/7 at low). The CPU needs idle periods to enter low-power states. Polling every second prevents this.

How should your app respond to .serious thermal state?

Disable non-essential features, stop prefetching, reduce image quality, and lower animation frame rates. At .serious, iOS is already throttling CPU/GPU — your app gets slower the more it demands.

Why is kCLLocationAccuracyBest a battery killer for most apps?

It activates GPS hardware continuously. A weather app only needs city-level accuracy (kCLLocationAccuracyKilometer). Use significantLocationChanges for background — it uses cell towers (low energy) instead of GPS.

What’s the difference between BGAppRefreshTask and BGProcessingTask?

BGAppRefreshTask is for quick syncs (~30s, <1 MB) that the system schedules optimally. BGProcessingTask is for heavy work (minutes) and can require external power + WiFi. Use processing tasks for ML training, database cleanup, or large downloads.

📌 Key Takeaways

  • Profile wirelessly — USB charging zeroes out Power Profiler metrics.
  • Power Profiler (Xcode 26) shows subsystem-level energy correlated with code execution.
  • MetricKit delivers real-world energy data daily. Compare across versions for regressions.
  • Top 5 drains: aggressive location, timer polling, background abuse, GPU overwork, uncompressed payloads.
  • Use significantLocationChanges over startUpdatingLocation for most apps.
  • Monitor ProcessInfo.thermalState and degrade gracefully at .serious and .critical.
  • BGAppRefreshTask: quick & light. BGProcessingTask: heavy + charging. Never mix them up.
