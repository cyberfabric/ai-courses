Chapter 5

Writing the PRD With AI (and Owning It)

Memory Map
✍️

The Big Idea

A PRD isn’t prose to be generated — it’s a sequence of decisions to be recorded. Write the headings yourself. Hand the AI one section at a time. Rewrite the two or three sentences that carry your judgment in your own voice. Authorship is decided by which calls you made, not by which keys you pressed.

🍳 The Mise en Place — PRD Anatomy (9 sections)

1️⃣
Problem
The friction, who feels it, evidence, why now.
2️⃣
Users
Cohort, segment, real account or ticket anchors.
3️⃣
Goals & Non-goals
What success means; what is explicitly out.
4️⃣
Scope
What ships in this PRD vs what comes later.
5️⃣
UX shape
High-level flow, not pixel design.
6️⃣
Acceptance criteria
Testable, observable conditions for “done”.
7️⃣
Risks & assumptions
What could break; what we’re betting on.
8️⃣
Metrics
How we’ll know in production it worked.
9️⃣
Open questions
What we don’t know yet, and who owns finding out.

Lock the outline. Stop renegotiating it weekly. The headings are your through-line.

🛣️ The Outline-First Workflow — Five Steps

  1. Write the 9 headings yourself

    On a blank page, in order. No AI yet.

  2. Type a one-sentence stake under each

    What that section will say. Rough is fine. ~30 seconds per heading. The single highest-leverage habit in this chapter.

  3. Paste outline + context pack into the AI

    Five context pieces (see below). Same five work in Claude / ChatGPT / Gemini / Copilot / Windsurf.

  4. Ask AI to expand one section

    Against your stake, in your structure. Not the whole PRD. Stop the model from sprinting ahead.

  5. Rewrite the judgment sentences in your voice

    The 2–3 sentences a stranger could not have produced. Move on to the next section.

🧱 Why "Give Me a Full PRD" Is the Wrong Unit

Aspect Full-PRD prompt Outline-first, one section
Output shape 9 sections of plausible generic prose 9 sections aimed at your stakes
Your posture Editor smoothing someone else’s draft Author with the AI as typist
Reads like Done at a glance, hollow on careful read Yours — because it is
"Why this scope?" You search for the answer in AI’s text You have a sentence ready you wrote
Wall-clock time ~Same ~Same

📦 The Context Pack — Five Pieces, ~1 Page

🏢
Product overview
One paragraph: what it is, who pays, core flow, current scale.
📒
Segments & glossary
Cohorts you talk about by name; internal terms (“workspace”, “owner”).
🔍
Discovery doc
Chosen candidate, central assumption, rejected options.
🚫
Prior decisions
3–5 “what we don’t do and why” lines. Saves the AI from suggesting ruled-out things.
📄
One similar prior PRD
A spec your team was happy with. AI learns house style faster from one example than from any prompt.

~90 seconds to paste. The single biggest determinant of section quality.

🔄 Three-Pass Refinement

🦴 Skeleton
AI fills against stake
Right ideas in right places?
→
🔪 Sharpen
Push back on generic
Replace “robust” with the failure mode
→
✨ Sand
Read aloud, cut, voice
Rewrite judgment sentences in your voice

Asking for “sharpened, polished” in one shot = looks finished, was never pressure-tested.

🎯 The Goals Section — Before & After

Aspect AI-fluent draft (says nothing) Your-voice rewrite (carries weight)
Numbers “Improve conversion” From 1.8% (dashboard-origin) toward 4.1% (marketing-origin)
Trade-off Implicit, hidden in “seamless” “Inline upgrade may feel pushy to a subset”
Falsification None “If dismissal exceeds 60% in week one, we hold and rethink”
Disagreeability Nothing concrete to disagree with A reviewer can push back on specifics

Disagreeable specifics are the proof that judgment was applied.

🚀 Where AI Is Genuinely Strong (Lean In)

⚠️
Filling Risks & Open Questions
Adversarial enumeration is its native skill. Will catch at least one risk you didn’t think of.
🔁
Translating between audiences
Re-voice a Goals section for the exec one-pager in 30 seconds, substance preserved.
🔪
Sharpening verbs
“Find every vague verb in this section and propose a specific behavior to replace it.”
🧮
Cross-checking consistency
“List every internal inconsistency between sections.” 10 seconds; catches embarrassing drift.

🚪 When to Close the Chat

📏 Section is small & the call is yours (Goals, Non-goals) → write 3–5 sentences first; AI pressure-tests after
🌫️ AI fluent on something with no real answer yet → mark it Open Question with an owner
Close the tab. Write by hand.
👥 Section is about people, not features → stakeholder notes, escalation paths — you alone

🪪 Versioning Discipline — Decision History

  1. One-line decision history at the top

    Five lines, ten seconds each. A late reviewer can read v0.1 → v0.5 and know the journey.

  2. Snapshot before each big edit

    Lets you back out an iteration that quietly weakened the through-line.

  3. One-line disclosure note at the top

    “Drafted with AI assistance. Section authorship and judgment are the PM’s.” Tool-honesty, not tool-blame.

📐 Scaling to the Size of the Bet

📝 Tactical brief
(2-day fix)
4 sections, ~30 min
→
⚙️ Sprint feature
(canonical)
9 sections, 3 passes, ~1–2 hr
→
🏔️ Quarter initiative
(strategic bet)
9 sections, multi-day, stakeholder log

Tactical depth on a strategic bet is the more dangerous mistake. When in doubt, slightly under-invest tactically, slightly over-invest strategically.

🩹 Six Common Stumbles

🌫️
1. AI drifts back to generic
Re-paste the discovery doc + prior PRD; re-ask for that one section. Anchor went stale.
🔀
2. Outline changes every PRD
Lock the 9 headings as your personal default. Tweak order; don’t reinvent weekly.
📈
3. Spec keeps growing, not converging
“Identify five sentences that could be removed without losing meaning.” Length signals confidence; conciseness signals care.
✏️
4. Editing AI prose, not authoring
Stakes are too thin. Strengthen them; the editing burden drops.
🪄
5. AI invents details that don’t exist
Glossary missing or thin. Add screen / setting / feature names. Hallucinations stop on next pass.
🪞
6. Reads weak next to a real PRD
Add one prior PRD to the context pack. Gap usually closes.

🤝 Co-Writing With a Teammate

  1. Share screen with the AI tool open

    PM types outline + stakes; teammate watches and reacts. AI = third participant.

  2. Teammate sharpens stakes first

    “Your stake says ‘ships behind a flag’ — which flag, who turns it on?”

  3. AI fills against sharpened stake; teammate reacts

    Engineer flags cost / feasibility / silent constraint AI couldn’t know.

  4. PM rewrites judgment sentences while teammate watches

    45 minutes → better PRD than 3 days solo. Domain knowledge in from the start.

Don’t let the AI become a tie-breaker. The model has no stake; it produces a polite middle path. Have the disagreement directly.

🪪 What "Owning It" Means

🛡️ You can defend any sentence without saying “the AI suggested it”
🎯 You named the 2–3 sentences that carry judgment — and wrote them in your voice
Authorship test (3 conditions)
🧪 You can describe what would change your mind — the falsification condition is yours

Authorship isn’t the percentage of words you typed. It’s which calls you made.

🚨 Smell Test for Finished Sections

✂️
The Removal Test
If I removed this sentence, would the PRD be meaningfully less informative? Sentences that survive carry weight.
📉
The 30–40% Cut
High-leverage PRDs end with 30–40% fewer sentences than first draft — and read sharper for it.
🪧
The Stranger Test
Could a stranger with full context have produced this sentence? If yes, it’s prose. If no, it’s judgment — must be in your voice.

📒 Key Terms

Outline-first workflow
Write 9 headings & one-sentence stakes yourself before involving AI; expand one section at a time. The whole technique in one phrase.
One-sentence stake
A rough sentence under each heading saying what that section will say. ~30 seconds per heading. The single highest-leverage habit in PRD work.
Context pack
Five pieces (product overview / glossary / discovery doc / prior decisions / one prior PRD) pasted at the start. Determines section quality more than any prompt.
Judgment sentences
The 2–3 sentences in a PRD that encode your trade-offs. A stranger couldn’t produce them. Must be in your voice; the AI can scaffold around them.
Three-pass refinement
Skeleton (right ideas?) → Sharpen (cut generics) → Sand (cut weight, voice). Each pass teaches you what to look for next time.
Decision history
One-line log at the top of the spec recording what changed between versions and why. Five minutes a week; enormous compound payoff.
Disclosure note
One-line top-of-spec note that AI helped draft. Tool-honesty, not tool-blame. Builds trust faster than silence.
Falsification condition
The signal that would prove the spec’s bet wrong (e.g. “dismissal > 60% in week one”). A spec without one is a wish; with one, a commitment.
Authorship test
Three conditions: defend any sentence, name the judgment sentences (in your voice), describe what would change your mind. Met ⇒ the spec is yours.

❓ Quick Quiz

What’s wrong with prompting for “a full PRD”?

It produces 9 sections of generic prose patterned on every other PRD the model has seen. Worse, it puts you in editor mode — smoothing sentences instead of authoring decisions. The artifact looks done at a glance and falls apart on careful read.

What is the “one-sentence stake” and why does it matter so much?

A rough sentence you type under each heading saying what that section will say. ~30 seconds per heading. Almost every “the AI gave me generic output” complaint dissolves when stakes are present — the model now knows what good looks like.

What are the five pieces of a context pack?

(1) Product overview, (2) segments & glossary, (3) discovery doc, (4) prior decisions / “what we don’t do” list, (5) one similar prior PRD for tone. ~1 page total. ~90 seconds to paste; dramatic quality lift.

What three passes does each section go through?

Skeleton (are the right ideas in the right places?) → Sharpen (push back on generic language) → Sand (read aloud, cut weight, rewrite judgment sentences in your voice). One-shot “polished” prompts skip all three.

Which sentences in the PRD must be in your voice?

The 2–3 that carry your judgment about your trade-offs — usually one in Goals (the trade-off being taken), one in Non-goals (what you’re saying no to), one in Scope (the cut), one in Risks (what you’d do if the bet fails). A stranger couldn’t have produced them, even with full context.

The three conditions of the authorship test?

(1) You can defend any sentence without invoking the AI. (2) You can name the judgment sentences and they’re in your voice. (3) You can describe what would change your mind. If yes to all three, the PRD is yours, even if AI drafted 80% of the words.

When should you close the AI tab and write by hand?

Three signals: (1) the section is small and the call is yours (Goals, Non-goals); (2) the AI is fluent on something with no real answer yet — mark it Open Question; (3) the section is about people, not features — stakeholder notes, escalation paths.

Why disclose AI assistance at the top of the spec?

A one-line note flips suspicion into explicit conversation, signals you’re not hiding the workflow, and gives future-you a record of which tools you used. Tool-honesty, not tool-blame. The silence usually leaks anyway — and leaks badly.

Key Takeaways

  • A PRD is a sequence of decisions, not prose to be generated. AI handles the wrapping; you make the decisions.
  • Write the 9 headings yourself. Type a one-sentence stake under each. That’s the highest-leverage habit in this chapter.
  • Prompt for one section at a time, against your stake, in your structure — not the whole PRD.
  • A 5-piece context pack (overview / glossary / discovery doc / prior decisions / one similar prior PRD) determines section quality more than any prompt.
  • Three passes per section: skeleton → sharpen → sand. One-shot “polished” prompts skip the pressure-test.
  • 2–3 sentences per PRD carry the judgment. Those must be in your voice with your specifics — the AI can scaffold around them.
  • Disagreeable specifics are proof that judgment was applied. “Robust”, “seamless”, “leverage” commit to nothing.
  • A one-line decision history + saved snapshots = five minutes a week, enormous payoff at review time.
  • A one-line top-of-spec disclosure of AI assistance builds trust faster than silence.
  • Lean into AI’s strengths: filling Risks, translating between audiences, sharpening verbs, cross-checking consistency.
  • Close the chat when: section is small & yours, no real answer yet, or section is about people not features.
  • Authorship test: defend any sentence, name the judgment sentences in your voice, describe what would change your mind.
