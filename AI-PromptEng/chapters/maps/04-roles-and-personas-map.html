Chapter 4

Role Play and Personas

Memory Map
💡

The Big Idea

"You are..." is like tuning a radio — all stations are always broadcasting, but the persona selects which one you hear. Without it, you get all stations at once: technically correct but lacking depth and focus.

🎭 Five Basic Role Types

🎓
Expert
Deep, technical, authoritative. Best for knowledge-heavy tasks.
🔍
Critic
Finds flaws, challenges assumptions. Best for reviews and QA.
📚
Teacher
Explains clearly, uses analogies. Best for learning and onboarding.
🎤
Interviewer
Asks probing questions. Best for prep and self-testing.
🌉
Translator
Converts jargon to plain language. Best for cross-team communication.

🎚️ The Specificity Spectrum

  1. Level 1: Generic

    "You are an expert." — Barely better than nothing.

  2. Level 2: Role

    "You are a senior Android developer." — Good. Activates platform + experience patterns.

  3. Level 3: Role + Context

    "...at a fintech company." — Better. Adds domain (security, compliance, financial UX).

  4. Level 4: Full Persona

    "...with 10 years of Kotlin, banking apps with millions of transactions, known for clean testable code." — Best. Hyper-focused output.

Quick questions: Level 2. Important decisions: Level 4.

👥 Ready-Made Team Personas

Role Focus Area Key Phrase
👨‍💻 Developer Clean code, SOLID, performance, testability "...writes clean, testable code and follows SOLID principles"
🧪 QA Engineer Edge cases, failure modes, boundary conditions "...known for finding bugs nobody else finds"
📋 PM/Manager User impact, cost, timeline, stakeholder communication "...bridges engineering and business"
🔒 Security Reviewer OWASP Top 10, attack vectors, data safety "...thinks like an attacker"

🛠️ Key Techniques

🎭
Role Stacking
Combine 2-3 roles: "Senior dev who also has backend + QA experience." Don't stack more than 3 — contradictions kill quality.
🎯
"Explain It To..."
Set the audience persona instead of the AI's. "Explain to a junior dev" vs "Explain to a tech lead" = completely different depth.
👥
Multi-Persona Debate
Simulate a team discussion: Developer + QA Lead + Manager each give their take. Like a mini meeting in one prompt.
💾
Persistent Personas
Save personas in CLAUDE.md, .cursorrules, ChatGPT custom instructions, or API system messages. Set once, reuse everywhere.

🚫 Anti-Patterns to Avoid

Over-Specified Persona
Personal details (age, hobbies, pets) waste tokens. Stick to role, expertise, experience, and priorities.
Contradictory Persona
"Risk-averse security expert AND move-fast startup engineer" — pick one priority or state the trade-off explicitly.
Flattery Persona
"World's greatest programmer" is useless. "Senior Kotlin dev focused on clean architecture" is actionable.

❓ Quick Quiz

Why does "You are a senior Android developer" produce better output than no persona?

It activates patterns associated with how senior Android developers write and think — like tuning a radio to a specific station instead of hearing all stations at once.

What's role stacking and what's its limit?

Combining multiple expertise areas in one persona (e.g., "developer with QA and backend experience"). Limit to 2-3 roles — more creates contradictions.

How does the "Explain it to..." technique differ from a regular persona?

Instead of setting who the AI IS, you set who the AUDIENCE is. The AI adjusts complexity and depth to match the audience level.

What makes a persona "over-specified"?

Including irrelevant personal details (age, hobbies, favorite coffee). Only role, expertise, experience, and priorities affect output quality.

When should you NOT use a persona?

For simple factual questions, basic code completion, quick conversions, or one-word answers. Personas add value when depth, nuance, or perspective matters.

📌 Key Takeaways

  • "You are..." activates role-specific knowledge patterns in the AI
  • Five basic roles: Expert, Critic, Teacher, Interviewer, Translator
  • More specificity = better output (Level 4 persona > Level 1 "expert")
  • Role stacking works with 2-3 roles; more creates contradictions
  • "Explain it to a [audience]" adjusts complexity without changing the AI's role
  • Save personas in CLAUDE.md / .cursorrules for reuse across conversations
  • Skip personas for simple, mechanical tasks — they add overhead without value
