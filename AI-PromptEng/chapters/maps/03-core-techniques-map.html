Chapter 3

Core Techniques: Zero-Shot, Few-Shot, and Chain-of-Thought

Memory Map
💡

The Big Idea

The building blocks (Ch 2) are the ingredients. These three techniques are the cooking methods. Same ingredients, completely different dishes. Start simple (zero-shot), upgrade as needed (few-shot), add reasoning for complex tasks (CoT).

🎯 The Three Techniques at a Glance

0️⃣
Zero-Shot
Just ask, no examples. Works for common tasks the AI has seen millions of times. Fast, simple, often good enough.
📋
Few-Shot
Show 2-5 examples first. AI infers the pattern. Essential for custom formats, company conventions, and consistency.
🧠
Chain-of-Thought
"Think step by step." Forces the AI to show intermediate reasoning. Dramatically improves math, logic, and analysis.
⚡
Few-Shot + CoT
The power combo. Show examples that include reasoning. AI learns both the format AND the thinking process.

📝 The Three Rules of Great Examples

  1. Diversity

    Show different variations, not three identical cases. Cover the full spectrum (high, medium, low).

  2. Edge Cases

    Include at least one tricky example. Teaches the AI to handle ambiguity, not just easy wins.

  3. Format Consistency

    Every example must follow the exact same format. Mixed formats confuse the AI.

Sweet spot: 2-5 examples. One = coincidence. Two = pattern. Three = confirmed. Five+ = usually overkill.

🧠 CoT Variants

Zero-Shot CoT
"Think step by step" — simple trigger, surprisingly effective, no examples needed.
Explicit CoT
"First analyze X, then evaluate Y, then recommend Z" — you define the reasoning steps.
Structured CoT
"Use this format: Observation, Analysis, Conclusion" — you template the thinking.
Few-Shot CoT
Show examples that include reasoning → AI learns both the pattern and the thinking.

✅ CoT: When It Helps vs Hurts

✅ Math & calculations
✅ Multi-step logic
CoT: "Think step by step"
❌ Simple factual Qs
❌ Straightforward formatting

Rule of thumb: if a human needs 10+ seconds to answer, CoT helps. If instant, skip it.

❓ Quick Quiz

When should you upgrade from zero-shot to few-shot?

When the output is wrong or inconsistent — especially when you need a specific format the AI can't guess (company templates, naming conventions).

Why does "think step by step" improve AI accuracy?

AI generates tokens one at a time. Intermediate reasoning tokens become context for the next step, letting the model "build momentum" instead of jumping from question to answer in one leap.

What makes a BAD set of few-shot examples?

All examples showing the same category/outcome (e.g., all "High" severity bugs). The AI can't learn the full spectrum from identical examples.

What is Few-Shot + CoT and when would you use it?

Showing examples that include reasoning steps. Use it when you need both consistent format AND good analytical thinking — like bug triage with impact analysis, or code migration with explanations.

When does CoT actually hurt?

Simple factual questions, straightforward formatting, translation, or when token budget is tight. CoT adds verbosity without improving simple tasks.

📌 Key Takeaways

  • Zero-shot = just ask. Works for common, unambiguous tasks.
  • Few-shot = show 2-5 examples. Essential for custom formats and consistency.
  • CoT = "think step by step." Dramatically improves math, logic, and complex analysis.
  • Few-shot + CoT = the power combo for format + reasoning tasks.
  • Good examples are diverse, include edge cases, and use consistent formatting.
  • Start simple (zero-shot), upgrade only when needed.
  • If a human needs 10+ seconds to think, the AI benefits from CoT.
