Prompting for Everyone: Writing, Analysis, and Planning

Memory Map

The Big Idea

Most of your workday isn't coding — it's emails, meetings, decisions, reports, and planning. Every one of these tasks follows the same prompting pattern: Task + Context + Specifics + Format + Quality Bar. The biggest untapped AI productivity gains are in non-code work.

🧰 The Non-Code Prompting Toolkit

📧
Email Drafting
Recipient + relationship + topic + tone + key points + constraints. Ask for 2-3 tone variations for tricky emails.
📋
Meeting Agendas
Duration + attendees + context + goals → structured agenda with time allocations and discussion questions.
🔍
Document Review
"What questions would a skeptical VP ask?" — the adversarial perspective trick for bulletproof proposals.
📊
Data Analysis
Data + specific questions + output structure (Pattern → Evidence → Hypothesis → Next Step).
⚖️
Decision Frameworks
Options + context + ranked priorities → pros/cons matrix + recommendation + hidden risks.
📝
Status Reports
Raw notes → Completed / In Progress / Blocked / Next Week. Two minutes, polished output.
🎤
Presentations
Audience + duration + goal + data points → per-slide outline with bullet points and speaker notes.
🎯
Interview Prep
Both sides: generate questions with green/yellow flags for interviewers, or mock interview mode for candidates.
📚
Learning & Research
The Learning Ladder: where I am + where I want to be + quiz questions to verify understanding.
🧩
Task Breakdown
Feature + team skills + dependencies → assigned tasks with effort estimates, order for max parallelism.

📊 Analysis Output Structure

  1. Pattern

    What trend or anomaly did you find in the data?

  2. Evidence

    What specific numbers support this pattern?

  3. Hypothesis

    What's the most likely explanation?

  4. Next Step

    What should we investigate or do about it?

⚖️ Decision-Making Power Moves

"What am I not considering?"
Forces AI to think beyond your framing and surface blind spots.
"Argue against my preferred option"
Free devil's advocate — stress-tests your leaning before you commit.
"If this fails, what's the most likely reason?"
Pre-mortem analysis — identifies risks while you can still mitigate them.
Ranked priorities
Always rank your decision criteria. "Ship fast" vs "low maintenance" changes the recommendation completely.
Pros/cons matrix
Ask for a table comparing options across your specific criteria, not generic dimensions.

👔 The Manager's Toolkit

Scenario Key Prompt Elements Watch Out For
1:1 Prep Situation context + open-ended questions + conversation structure + clear next step Tone: "supportive but honest" — not accusatory
Performance Review Specific observations + impact on team + concrete improvement suggestions ⚠️ Never paste real names/details into public AI tools

⚠️ Three Mistakes That Kill Non-Code Prompts

Mistake Why It Fails Fix
No audience specified A summary for the CEO ≠ a summary for the tech lead Always state who will read/hear the output
No tone set AI defaults to corporate-speak: "We sincerely apologize..." "Tone: direct and transparent, no corporate speak"
Accepting first draft AI output is a starting point, not a final product Iterate: "Too formal — make it warmer" or "Cut to top 3 findings"

❓ Quick Quiz

What's the single most important instruction for email prompts?

Tone. "Professional but collaborative" produces vastly different output than "direct and urgent." Without a tone instruction, AI defaults to generic corporate language.

What's the "skeptical VP" trick and why does it work?

Ask "What questions would a skeptical VP ask?" when reviewing documents. It forces the AI to think adversarially — the exact perspective you need before presenting to stakeholders. Works with any role: "skeptical security reviewer," "impatient CTO," etc.

What are the three components of the Learning Ladder?

(1) Where I am — "I understand threads but not coroutines." (2) Where I want to be — "I need to use coroutines in production." (3) How to check — "Give me quiz questions to test my understanding." The quiz questions force the right depth.

How do you turn AI into a mock interviewer?

After generating interview questions, follow up with: "Now act as the interviewer. Ask me these questions one at a time. After each answer, give me feedback on what was strong and what I could improve. Be honest, not encouraging."

Why should you never paste employee details into public AI tools?

Privacy risk. Names, performance data, and HR details in public AI tools could be stored, leaked, or used for training. Use your company's approved enterprise AI tools with data protection, or anonymize all details before prompting.

📌 Key Takeaways

  • The Universal Pattern works for everything: Task + Context + Specifics + Format + Quality Bar
  • Email formula: recipient + topic + tone + key points + constraints (tone is #1)
  • For analysis: always specify questions AND output structure (Pattern → Evidence → Hypothesis → Next Step)
  • Decision prompts: rank priorities, ask for a matrix, and add "what am I not considering?"
  • Status reports: raw notes + format template = polished update in 2 minutes
  • The Learning Ladder: where I am + where I want to be + quiz to verify
  • Mock interview mode: "Ask me one at a time, give honest feedback after each"
  • Never accept first drafts — iterate on tone, length, and focus
  • Never paste sensitive employee data into public AI tools
